# Document Retention Policy
## Nouns Foundation
**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

---

## §1. Purpose

This Policy governs the retention, storage, and destruction of records of Nouns Foundation, Inc. (the "Corporation"), a Delaware nonprofit nonstock corporation operated as a public charity within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (the "**Code**"). Sound records management serves four objectives the Corporation considers indivisible: (a) compliance with federal and Delaware recordkeeping obligations; (b) preservation of evidence supporting the Corporation's tax-exempt status, public-charity classification, and grantmaking determinations; (c) operational continuity across Director transitions, officer turnover, and changes in service providers; and (d) defensibility in the event of audit, examination, investigation, or litigation. This Policy implements those objectives through a uniform retention schedule, defined storage standards, controlled destruction procedures, and an enforceable litigation hold.

## §2. Scope

This Policy applies to **all records** created, received, or maintained by or on behalf of the Corporation, regardless of medium. Covered records include paper documents, electronic files, email and other electronic communications, documents stored in cloud services (including Google Workspace), database records, blockchain transaction records ("**On-Chain Records**"), off-chain documentation supporting on-chain transactions, photographs, recordings, and any other tangible or intangible record of Corporation activity. This Policy applies to records held by Directors, officers, employees (if any), contractors, vendors, and any other person acting on behalf of the Corporation.

## §3. Definitions

For purposes of this Policy:

§3.1. "**Records**" means information of any kind, in any medium, that documents the Corporation's organization, governance, operations, finances, grantmaking, or compliance, and that is maintained by or on behalf of the Corporation in the ordinary course.

§3.2. "**Originals**" means the authoritative version of a Record. For documents executed in counterparts or with electronic signatures, the fully executed instrument constitutes the Original. For Records created natively in electronic form (including On-Chain Records), the electronic version is the Original.

§3.3. "**Copies**" means duplicates of an Original Record, including photocopies, scanned images, exported PDFs, and replicated files.

§3.4. "**Active Records**" means Records currently in use for ongoing operations, accessible to authorized personnel in the Corporation's primary records systems.

§3.5. "**Archived Records**" means Records no longer in active use but retained for the balance of their applicable retention period, stored in a manner that preserves their integrity and permits retrieval if needed.

§3.6. "**On-Chain Records**" means transaction records recorded on a public blockchain in connection with the Corporation's operations, including (without limitation) transaction hashes, multisig signer changes, smart-contract interactions, and the on-chain text of any DAO governance proposal that recommends or relates to a Corporation Grant.

§3.7. "**Off-Chain Supporting Documentation**" means documentation maintained by the Corporation outside any blockchain that supports, contextualizes, or evidences the charitable purpose, diligence basis, recipient identity, or Board approval underlying an On-Chain Record. Examples include grant memos, KYC files, recipient diligence packages, signed grant agreements, and Board minutes or written consents authorizing a transaction.

§3.8. "**Litigation Hold**" means a directive issued under §8 of this Policy suspending the routine destruction of specified Records in connection with actual, pending, or reasonably anticipated litigation, audit, examination, or government investigation.

§3.9. "**Retention Period**" means the period of time, as set forth in §4 or as extended by a Litigation Hold, during which a Record must be retained.

§3.10. "**Records Officer**" means the officer designated by the Board under §9 with primary responsibility for administration of this Policy.

## §5. On-Chain Records and Supporting Documentation

The Corporation operates an on-chain treasury (currently a multisig wallet, expected to be implemented on Safe or a comparable platform) and may approve and disburse Grants on-chain. On-Chain Records present three records-management features that warrant special handling:

§5.1. **Immutability of the on-chain record.** Transaction hashes, smart-contract calls, and multisig signer events recorded on a public blockchain are immutable in their on-chain form. The Corporation's affirmative retention obligation for these Records is satisfied by the persistence of the underlying chain, but the Corporation shall maintain an off-chain archived copy of each material on-chain transaction (hash, block height, contract address, calldata summary, timestamp, and counterparty identification) so that the Corporation's records remain accessible if the relevant chain or indexing service becomes unavailable.

§5.2. **Insufficiency of the on-chain record alone.** The on-chain transaction hash establishes that a transfer occurred. It does not establish the charitable purpose served, the identity of the recipient, the diligence performed, or the Board approval underlying the transaction. The Corporation shall maintain Off-Chain Supporting Documentation for every material on-chain transaction, including (as applicable) the grant memo, the recipient KYC file, the signed grant agreement, the Board approval (meeting minute or written consent), and any reports received from the recipient. Off-Chain Supporting Documentation is retained per §4.

§5.3. **DAO governance proposal text.** Where a Corporation Grant is associated with a Nouns DAO Charitable Recommendation Proposal, the Corporation shall retain an off-chain archived copy of the proposal text, the on-chain proposal identifier, the voting outcome, and any propdates, commentary, or amendments material to the Corporation's evaluation of the related Grant. This documentation supports the Corporation's compliance posture under the Grantmaking Policy and forms part of the diligence file for the related Grant.

The Records Officer shall maintain an On-Chain Records archive (in the Corporation's primary records system) that crosswalks each material on-chain transaction to its Off-Chain Supporting Documentation. This crosswalk shall be reconciled no less frequently than quarterly.

## §6. Storage and Format

§6.1. **Primary records system.** The Corporation's primary records system is Google Workspace (Drive, Docs, Sheets), supplemented by the on-chain record where applicable. All Records shall be stored in the primary records system unless this Policy or a Litigation Hold directs otherwise.

§6.2. **Acceptable formats.** Records may be stored in their native format (paper or electronic). Paper Records of permanent or seven-year retention significance shall be scanned to PDF and stored in the primary records system; the scanned copy may serve as the Original where applicable law and the document's character permit. Where applicable law requires retention of a wet-ink Original (for example, certain executed real-property instruments), the wet-ink Original shall be retained in a secure off-site location and indexed in the primary records system.

§6.3. **Backup.** The Corporation's electronic records are subject to the backup features of Google Workspace. The Records Officer shall additionally cause an off-platform backup of the primary records system to be made no less frequently than quarterly, retained for the longer of one year or the next quarterly backup, in a manner that protects against vendor failure, account compromise, or accidental deletion.

§6.4. **Sensitive records.** Records containing personally identifiable information, KYC documentation, donor-identifying information not intended for public disclosure, banking information, attorney-client privileged communications, and any other sensitive Records shall be stored with access limited to those with a need to know and protected by encryption at rest and in transit. Access to sensitive Records is logged where the storage platform supports logging.

§6.5. **Records held by third parties.** Where Records are held by third-party service providers (auditor, accountant, legal counsel, KYC vendor, payroll provider), the Corporation shall obtain confirmation, in the engagement letter or by separate writing, that the provider's retention practices meet or exceed the Corporation's Retention Periods, or shall obtain copies of those Records for retention in the primary records system.

§6.6. **Format obsolescence.** The Records Officer shall, no less frequently than annually, assess the long-term readability of Archived Records and shall migrate Records from obsolete or at-risk formats to current standard formats as needed.

## §7. Destruction Procedures

§7.1. **No destruction before end of Retention Period.** No Record may be destroyed before the end of its applicable Retention Period. Routine deletion of duplicate Copies and ephemeral working files (e.g., interim drafts within a single working session, cached files) does not constitute destruction for purposes of this Policy.

§7.2. **Authorization.** Destruction of Records at the end of the applicable Retention Period requires written authorization by the Records Officer, after confirmation that no Litigation Hold applies to the Record category.

§7.3. **Destruction log.** The Records Officer shall maintain a destruction log, retained permanently, recording for each authorized destruction:

- the Record category;
- the date range of Records destroyed;
- the date of destruction;
- the method of destruction; and
- the signature (electronic or wet-ink) of the Records Officer authorizing destruction.

§7.4. **Methods of destruction.** Destruction shall be performed by methods appropriate to the medium and the sensitivity of the Records:

§7.4.1. Paper Records containing sensitive information: cross-cut shredding or commercial secure-destruction service with certificate of destruction;

§7.4.2. Paper Records not containing sensitive information: any reasonable disposal method;

§7.4.3. Electronic Records: deletion from the primary records system, all backups within the Records Officer's control, and any device-level copies, using methods reasonably designed to prevent recovery; and

§7.4.4. Records on third-party systems: written instruction to the third party to destroy, with confirmation requested where reasonably available.

§7.5. **On-Chain Records.** On-Chain Records cannot be destroyed; the Corporation's destruction obligation, where applicable, applies only to Off-Chain Supporting Documentation. Destruction of Off-Chain Supporting Documentation at the end of its Retention Period proceeds under this Section.


## §8. Litigation Hold

§8.1. **Anti-destruction obligations.** Two provisions of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 apply to nonprofit corporations notwithstanding that most of Sarbanes-Oxley addresses public companies:

§8.1.1. **Section 1102** (codified at 18 U.S.C. § 1512(c)) makes it a federal crime to corruptly alter, destroy, mutilate, or conceal a record, document, or other object — or attempt to do so — with the intent to impair its integrity or availability for use in an official proceeding.

§8.1.2. **Section 1519** (codified at 18 U.S.C. § 1519) makes it a federal crime to knowingly alter, destroy, mutilate, conceal, cover up, falsify, or make a false entry in any record, document, or tangible object with the intent to impede, obstruct, or influence the investigation or proper administration of any matter within the jurisdiction of any federal department or agency, or in relation to or contemplation of any such matter.

These provisions, together with analogous Delaware and other state law, require that the Corporation suspend routine destruction of Records the moment it has notice of any actual, pending, or reasonably anticipated investigation, audit, examination, or litigation.

§8.2. **Issuance of Litigation Hold.** Upon the Corporation's receipt of any of the following, the Records Officer (in consultation with legal counsel) shall promptly issue a written Litigation Hold:

- a subpoena, civil investigative demand, audit notice, examination notice, or similar government request;
- a complaint, demand letter, preservation letter, or other communication credibly threatening litigation; or
- the Corporation's own determination that litigation, audit, or investigation is reasonably anticipated.

§8.3. **Content of Litigation Hold.** A Litigation Hold shall:

- identify the matter to which the Hold relates;
- describe the categories of Records subject to the Hold;
- identify the Directors, officers, employees, contractors, and other custodians required to preserve;
- instruct each custodian to suspend any destruction (whether routine or otherwise) of Records within the scope of the Hold; and
- instruct each custodian to acknowledge receipt of the Hold in writing.

§8.4. **Effect.** During the pendency of a Litigation Hold, no Record within the scope of the Hold may be destroyed, regardless of the otherwise applicable Retention Period. The Records Officer shall maintain a log of Litigation Holds in effect.

§8.5. **Release.** A Litigation Hold may be released only by the Records Officer in consultation with legal counsel, by written notice to all custodians who received the Hold. Upon release, Records previously subject to the Hold revert to their normal Retention Periods (and may be destroyed if their Retention Period has by then expired, unless other Holds apply).

§8.6. **Notification of legal counsel.** The Records Officer shall notify the Corporation's legal counsel promptly upon any event that triggers or is reasonably likely to trigger a Litigation Hold. Where the Corporation does not have standing legal counsel for the relevant matter, the Records Officer shall notify the Board Chair, who shall arrange for appropriate counsel.

## §9. Compliance and Annual Review

§9.1. **Records Officer.** The Board shall designate an officer of the Corporation as the Records Officer. The Treasurer shall serve as the Records Officer unless the Board designates another officer. The Records Officer is responsible for the administration of this Policy, including maintenance of the destruction log, issuance and tracking of Litigation Holds, oversight of storage and backup, and the annual review under §9.2.

§9.2. **Annual review.** No less frequently than annually, the Records Officer shall:

- review the Records Retention Schedule against then-current federal, Delaware, and other applicable law and recommend any amendments to the Board;
- review the Corporation's storage and backup arrangements;
- review compliance with this Policy across Directors, officers, employees, and contractors; and
- report findings to the Board.

§9.3. **Board approval of changes.** Material changes to the Records Retention Schedule or to this Policy require approval by the Board. Non-material clarifications may be made by the Records Officer with notice to the Board.

§9.4. **Training.** The Records Officer shall ensure that each new Director, officer, employee, and material contractor receives a copy of this Policy and acknowledges receipt in writing.

## §10. Amendment

This Policy may be amended, in whole or in part, by resolution of the Board, provided that no amendment shall be inconsistent with the Bylaws, the Certificate of Incorporation, or applicable law. Amendments shall be reflected in the Amendment History table at the head of this Policy.
