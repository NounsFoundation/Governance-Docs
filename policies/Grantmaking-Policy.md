# Grantmaking Policy

## Nouns Foundation

**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

---

## §1. Purpose

The Corporation's mission is to advance the public good through charitable grants and related activities in four areas:

- **Civic engagement and democratic participation** — supporting the study, development, and deployment of decentralized governance technologies and other initiatives that increase informed public participation in governance and strengthen civic institutions;
- **Public goods, science, and technology** — advancing science and education through grants for open-source infrastructure, freely-licensed public technologies, and scientific research, including research conducted through decentralized or open-collaboration models;
- **Arts and culture** — supporting the creation, presentation, and preservation of artistic and cultural works, including grants to individual artists and arts organizations; and
- **Community welfare** — supporting grassroots, community-based organizations and direct-impact initiatives that promote social welfare and serve communities and people in need.

This Policy:

§1.1. defines what constitutes an eligible grant under the Corporation's charitable mission;

§1.2. describes how grant ideas are sourced, including the procedure under which Nouns DAO communicates non-binding recommendations to the Corporation;

§1.3. calibrates due diligence requirements to the size and risk profile of each grant;

§1.4. reserves approval of every Grant to the Board of Directors, with a Written Consent mechanism for between-meeting approval to accommodate the timing of incoming Charitable Recommendation Proposals;

§1.5. establishes documentation standards that support the Corporation's compliance posture under Form 990, the public support test, and the private-benefit and self-dealing rules; and

§1.6. coordinates with the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy, Gift Acceptance Policy, Investment Policy Statement, and the Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and Nouns DAO.

## §2. Definitions

For purposes of this Policy:

§2.1. "**Grant**" means any disbursement of funds, transfer of assets, forgiveness of indebtedness, or other transfer of value by the Corporation to a third party in furtherance of the Corporation's exempt purposes, but does not include compensation paid for goods or services received by the Corporation in arm's-length transactions.

§2.2. "**Grant Recommendation**" means a non-binding recommendation communicated by Nouns DAO to the Corporation through the procedure described in §5 (Recommendation Flow).

§2.3. "**Charitable Recommendation Proposal**" means a Nouns DAO governance proposal communicating a Grant Recommendation.

§2.4. "**Public Charity Recipient**" means a recipient that is, at the time of the grant, an organization described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Code and classified as a public charity under Section 509(a)(1), 509(a)(2), or 509(a)(3) (Type I, II, or functionally integrated Type III) of the Code, in good standing on the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search and not listed on any United States Office of Foreign Assets Control sanctions list.

§2.5. "**Non-501(c)(3) Recipient**" means a recipient that is not a Public Charity Recipient, including (without limitation) governmental units, foreign charitable organizations, fiscal sponsors acting on behalf of unincorporated projects, individuals (subject to §10), and for-profit entities receiving grants for charitable purposes.

§2.6. "**Expenditure Responsibility**" means the procedures required under Section 4945(h) of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4945-5 for grants to Non-501(c)(3) Recipients (including pre-grant inquiry, written grant agreement with required terms, periodic and final reporting, and IRS reporting on Form 990).

§2.7. "**Recommendation Proposer**" means, with respect to any Grant Recommendation, a person who:

- submitted the Charitable Recommendation Proposal communicating the Grant Recommendation;
- is named as a co-proposer on that Charitable Recommendation Proposal; or
- actively championed passage of the Charitable Recommendation Proposal through public advocacy, sponsored discussion, or organized voting support.

§2.8. "**Written Consent**" means an action of the Board taken between regular or special meetings, by written instrument signed by the required number of Directors, in accordance with §7.2 of this Policy and applicable law.

§2.9. "**Grants Lead**" means the Officer of the Corporation designated by the Board under §4 (and pursuant to the Grants Lead provisions of the Bylaws) to coordinate the operational work of the Corporation's grantmaking program.

§2.10. "**Independent Director**" means a Director who satisfies all four of the following conditions throughout the relevant period:

§2.10.1. The Director did not receive compensation as an officer or other employee of the Corporation or any related organization. Reasonable compensation paid to the Director for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board does not affect this analysis.

§2.10.2. The Director did not receive total compensation or other payments from the Corporation and any related organizations as an independent contractor exceeding $10,000 during the year. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses under an accountable plan, and reasonable compensation for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board, are not counted toward the $10,000 threshold.

§2.10.3. Neither the Director nor any family member of the Director was involved in a transaction with the Corporation, whether directly or indirectly through affiliation with another organization, of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Corporation's Form 990 (Transactions with Interested Persons).

§2.10.4. Neither the Director nor any family member of the Director was involved in a transaction, whether directly or indirectly, with a taxable or tax-exempt related organization of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Form 990 of that related organization.

For clauses §2.10.3 and §2.10.4, Schedule L transactions include excess benefit transactions, loans to or from interested persons, grants or assistance benefiting interested persons, and business transactions involving interested persons. The fact that a Director is a donor to the Corporation, in any amount, does not by itself affect the Director's status as an Independent Director. This definition is intended to track the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the Internal Revenue Service instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b, as those instructions may be amended from time to time. In the event of any inconsistency between this definition and the Form 990 instructions then in effect, the Form 990 instructions govern for purposes of the Corporation's Form 990 reporting.

## §3. Eligible Recipients and Charitable Purposes

§3.1. **Charitable purposes.** The Corporation may make Grants for any purpose described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, including (without limitation) relief of the poor, the distressed, or the underprivileged; advancement of religion, education, or science; erection or maintenance of public buildings, monuments, or works; lessening the burdens of government; lessening neighborhood tensions; eliminating prejudice and discrimination; defending human and civil rights secured by law; combating community deterioration and juvenile delinquency; or any combination of the foregoing.

§3.2. **Charitable class.** Each Grant shall benefit a charitable class — that is, a group of persons that is sufficiently large and indefinite that it constitutes the public, or a community, rather than a pre-selected group of identified individuals.

§3.3. **Eligible recipient types.** The Corporation may make Grants to Public Charity Recipients without Expenditure Responsibility, and to Non-501(c)(3) Recipients with Expenditure Responsibility as required under §8.

§3.4. **Geographic scope.** The Corporation may make Grants to recipients operating in the United States or in any foreign jurisdiction, subject to:

- §9 (Foreign Grants);
- compliance with United States economic sanctions; and
- any geographic limitations the Board may from time to time adopt.

§3.5. **Categorical exclusions.** The Corporation shall not make Grants:

§3.5.1. that would result in private inurement to any insider of the Corporation;
§3.5.2. that would result in more-than-incidental private benefit to any person;
§3.5.3. for political campaign intervention or substantial lobbying;
§3.5.4. to any person or entity on a United States economic sanctions list;
§3.5.5. for any purpose that is not described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Code; or
§3.5.6. for any purpose that, in the Board's judgment, would create undue compliance, examination, or reputational risk to the Corporation.

## §4. Grants Lead

§4.1. **Designation; Officer status.** The Grants Lead is an Officer position of the Corporation, designated by the Board pursuant to the Grants Lead provisions of the Bylaws. The Grants Lead serves at the pleasure of the Board and may be redesignated, replaced, or removed by the Board at any time, with or without cause.

§4.2. **Term.** The Grants Lead serves a one-year term beginning on designation, with rotation flexibility. The Board may, by resolution, redesignate the same person for additional one-year terms or rotate the role annually. If a successor has not been designated at the end of a term, the incumbent Grants Lead continues to serve until a successor is designated or the role is left vacant by Board action under §4.8.

§4.3. **Eligibility.** The Grants Lead must be a Director, consistent with the Officer eligibility provisions of the Bylaws. The Grants Lead is not required to be an Independent Director, although the Board may consider independence as a factor in designation.

§4.4. **Compensation.** The Grants Lead is eligible for compensation under the Compensation Review Process if the Board so determines, subject to the rebuttable presumption procedure described in the Officer compensation provisions of the Bylaws and the Compensation Review Process — the safe harbor under Section 4958 of the Code (independent body review, comparability data, contemporaneous documentation) that, when followed, causes the IRS to presume the compensation is reasonable.

§4.5. **Responsibilities.** The Grants Lead is responsible for the operational coordination of the Corporation's grantmaking program. Specifically, the Grants Lead shall:

§4.5.1. Monitor the Nouns DAO governance process for Charitable Recommendation Proposals and receive notice of passage on behalf of the Corporation;

§4.5.2. Conduct, or coordinate the conduct of, the initial screening of Charitable Recommendation Proposals under §5.6;

§4.5.3. Conduct, or coordinate the conduct of, the due diligence procedures under §6 for each Grant under evaluation;

§4.5.4. Prepare a written grant memo for each Grant under evaluation, summarizing the diligence performed, the recommended action, and any conditions or modifications recommended;

§4.5.5. Distribute Written Consent packages to Directors under §7.2.2, or place Grants on the agenda of regular or special meetings of the Board for consideration;

§4.5.6. Communicate with grant recipients before, during, and after Board action;

§4.5.7. Communicate the disposition of each Grant Recommendation to the Nouns DAO community under §5.9;

§4.5.8. Maintain the diligence file for each Grant under §6.6 and the Grant Register under §12.3;

§4.5.9. Report to the Board at each regular meeting on grantmaking activity, pending evaluations, and any operational matters warranting Board attention; and

§4.5.10. Coordinate with the Treasurer and the Board Chair on matters that intersect their respective areas of responsibility.

§4.6. **No independent approval authority.** The Grants Lead has no independent authority to approve, decline, or modify any Grant. All Grant approvals require Board action under §7. The Grants Lead's recommendations to the Board are non-binding.

§4.7. **Outside resources.** The Grants Lead may engage outside resources (background-check vendors, sanctions-screening services, recipient-engagement support, legal counsel for specific grant questions) within a budget approved by the Board, and may delegate operational work to other staff or contractors as appropriate. The Grants Lead remains responsible to the Board for the work delegated.

§4.8. **Vacancy.** If the Grants Lead resigns from the role or ceases to serve as an Officer, the Board shall designate a successor at its next regular or special meeting. Pending designation of a successor, the Board Chair may perform the Grants Lead's responsibilities or may designate an interim Grants Lead by written communication to the full Board.

## §5. Sources of Grant Ideas; Recommendation Flow

§5.1. **Multiple sources.** The Corporation may identify, evaluate, and approve Grants based on candidates identified through any of the following sources, on equal footing:

§5.1.1. Grant Recommendations from Nouns DAO under this §5;
§5.1.2. Inbound applications submitted to the Corporation through its application process;
§5.1.3. Recommendations from current or former Directors, officers, or staff;
§5.1.4. Outbound search and outreach by the Corporation; and
§5.1.5. Any other source the Grants Lead considers appropriate.

The Grants Lead shall not give preference or weight to any candidate based on the source of the recommendation, but shall evaluate each candidate on the merits under this Policy.

§5.2. **Recommendation Flow from Nouns DAO.** Nouns DAO may, from time to time, communicate Grant Recommendations to the Corporation through the passage of a Charitable Recommendation Proposal under the standard governance process of Nouns DAO. As of the effective date of this Policy, Nouns DAO conducts governance through the Nouns DAO Governor smart contract deployed at `0x6f3E6272A167e8AcCb32072d08E0957F9c79223d` on Ethereum mainnet, with proxy implementation upgradable under Nouns DAO's existing governance procedures (the "**Nouns DAO Governor**"). In the event Nouns DAO upgrades, replaces, or forks the contract or contracts through which it conducts its governance, the Corporation shall recognize as the operative Nouns DAO Governor those contracts identified as such by the Nouns DAO Administrators acting under Wyo. Stat. §§ 17-32-101 *et seq.* and the Nouns DAO Bylaws. The Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and Nouns DAO further describes the framework for this recommendation flow.

§5.3. **Required content of a Charitable Recommendation Proposal.** A Charitable Recommendation Proposal shall include the following public-safe content:

§5.3.1. A description of the proposed charitable activity;
§5.3.2. The type of recipient (e.g., Public Charity Recipient, governmental unit, foreign charity, individual under Section 4945(g), fiscal-sponsor-supported project, etc.);
§5.3.3. The proposed amount and currency;
§5.3.4. The proposed use of funds, with sufficient specificity to enable the Corporation's evaluation;
§5.3.5. The charitable class served;
§5.3.6. Any disclosed conflicts of interest of the proposer or supporters; and
§5.3.7. A commitment to report on use of funds consistent with the Corporation's reporting requirements.

§5.4. **Two-Layer Submission.** Personally identifiable information regarding the proposed recipient — including legal name, address, EIN (if applicable), KYC documentation, banking information, and any other information required for the Corporation's diligence under this Policy — shall flow directly to the Corporation through the private intake process described in §5.5, separately from the public on-chain Charitable Recommendation Proposal.

§5.5. **Private intake process.** Following passage of a Charitable Recommendation Proposal, the Corporation shall communicate to the Recommendation Proposer (or to the Nouns DAO Administrators, as appropriate) the private-intake instructions for the proposed recipient, including required documentation, KYC procedures, and any initial diligence questions. The Corporation may rely on KYC and identity-verification infrastructure operated by the Nouns DAO Administrators (including Persona or any successor service) to support its diligence.

§5.6. **Initial screening.** Within fifteen (15) business days following passage of a Charitable Recommendation Proposal, the Grants Lead shall conduct an initial screening to confirm:

§5.6.1. The proposed Grant is eligible under §3;
§5.6.2. The required content under §5.3 is present;
§5.6.3. No categorical exclusion under §3.5 applies; and
§5.6.4. No facial issue exists that would prevent further evaluation.

If any of the foregoing fails, the Grants Lead shall communicate to the Nouns DAO community (through a posting on the Corporation's website, a propdate, or other appropriate means) that the recommendation has been declined at initial screening, with a brief statement of the basis for declination.

§5.7. **Full evaluation.** Recommendations passing initial screening shall proceed to full evaluation under §6 (Due Diligence) and to Board approval under §7.

§5.8. **No obligation to accept.** The Corporation is not obligated to accept any Grant Recommendation. The Board, acting through the appropriate approval authority under §7, may approve, modify, condition, or decline any Grant Recommendation in the exercise of its independent fiduciary discretion. The reasons for declination need not exceed what this Policy requires.

§5.9. **Communication of disposition.** Following final disposition of a Grant Recommendation (whether approval, conditional approval, modification, declination, or withdrawal), the Corporation shall communicate the disposition to the Nouns DAO community through:

- a posting on the Corporation's website summarizing the disposition; or
- a propdate to the Nouns DAO governance forum.

## §6. Due Diligence

Due diligence requirements are organized by the type of recipient and by any heightened compliance required for the Grant. Every Grant must satisfy the baseline diligence in §6.1. Additional diligence in §6.2 through §6.5 applies based on the recipient or the nature of the Grant.

§6.1. **Baseline diligence — every Grant.** For every Grant, the Corporation shall:

§6.1.1. Confirm the recipient's identity and (for organizational recipients) legal status;
§6.1.2. Confirm that the recipient is not on any United States economic sanctions list (OFAC, SDN);
§6.1.3. Document the charitable purpose served;
§6.1.4. Document the charitable class served;
§6.1.5. Confirm that no Director, officer, or key employee has a recusal-triggering conflict under the Conflict of Interest Policy that has not been disclosed and managed;
§6.1.6. Confirm that the Grant does not violate any categorical exclusion under §3.5;
§6.1.7. Document the source of the grant idea (including any related Charitable Recommendation Proposal); and
§6.1.8. Conduct a brief media and reputation check.

§6.2. **Additional diligence — Public Charity Recipients.** For Grants to Public Charity Recipients, the Corporation shall additionally:

§6.2.1. Confirm 501(c)(3) status and public charity classification through the IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search; and
§6.2.2. Review the recipient's most recent Form 990 (or 990-EZ or 990-PF) for any indication of compliance issues or financial distress.

§6.3. **Additional diligence — domestic governmental units.** For Grants to domestic governmental units, the Corporation shall additionally:

§6.3.1. Confirm the unit's governmental status and authority to receive the Grant; and
§6.3.2. Document the public purpose served by the Grant.

§6.4. **Additional diligence — Grants requiring Expenditure Responsibility.** For Grants to Non-501(c)(3) Recipients other than domestic governmental units, the Corporation shall comply with the Expenditure Responsibility procedures set forth in §8.

§6.5. **Additional diligence — foreign and individual Grants.** Grants to foreign recipients are subject to the heightened procedures in §9. Grants to individuals are subject to the heightened procedures in §10.

§6.6. **Diligence file.** The Corporation shall maintain a diligence file for each Grant, retained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

## §7. Approval by the Board

§7.1. **General rule.** Every Grant requires approval by the Board of Directors. Approval may be given at a regular or special meeting of the Board, or by Written Consent under §7.2.

§7.2. **Approval by Written Consent.**

§7.2.1. **Permitted use.** Because Charitable Recommendation Proposals from Nouns DAO may pass at any time and may carry timing considerations that do not align with the Board's regular meeting cadence, the Board may approve any Grant by Written Consent between regular or special meetings.

§7.2.2. **Form.** The Grants Lead (under §4) or the Board Chair shall distribute to all Directors a Written Consent package consisting of:

- a written consent instrument;
- the diligence memo for the Grant; and
- any related documents needed for Director evaluation.

§7.2.3. **Required signatures.** Approval by Written Consent requires the signatures of a majority of the Directors then in office, returned within the response period set forth in the Written Consent package (which shall not be less than five (5) business days from distribution).

§7.2.4. **Director's right to require meeting consideration.** Any Director may, within the response period, decline to sign the Written Consent and request that the Grant be brought to a regular or special meeting of the Board for consideration. Upon any such request, the Written Consent process for that Grant is suspended and the Grant shall be considered at the next regular Board meeting or at a special meeting called for the purpose. The requesting Director need not provide reasons.

§7.2.5. **Notification of all Directors.** All Directors, including any who have not yet signed and any who declined to sign, shall be notified contemporaneously of the action taken or, if the Written Consent did not pass, of that outcome.

§7.2.6. **Documentation.** Each Written Consent shall be filed with the records of the Corporation and shall be reflected in the minutes of the next regular Board meeting.

§7.3. **Mandatory meeting consideration.** Notwithstanding §7.2, the following Grants require approval at a regular or special meeting of the Board and may not be approved by Written Consent:

§7.3.1. Any Grant in which a Director, officer, or key employee of the Corporation is a Recommendation Proposer, has a financial interest in the recipient, or has any other recusal-triggering conflict under the Conflict of Interest Policy. The Board's consideration shall include the recused person's absence from the deliberation and vote, in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy.

§7.3.2. Any Grant the Board Chair, the Grants Lead, or any Director has determined warrants synchronous Board discussion.

§7.4. **Approval threshold.** Whether by meeting vote or by Written Consent, approval of a Grant requires the affirmative vote (or signature) of a majority of the Directors then in office (excluding any Director recused under the Conflict of Interest Policy).

§7.5. **Board oversight.** Approvals by Written Consent do not relieve the Board of its ongoing oversight responsibility. The Board retains authority to review and, prior to disbursement, modify or rescind any Written Consent approval at any subsequent regular or special meeting.

## §8. Expenditure Responsibility

For each Grant requiring Expenditure Responsibility, the Corporation shall comply with the procedures set forth in Section 4945(h) of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4945-5, including:

§8.1. **Pre-grant inquiry.** A reasonable inquiry into the identity, prior history, and management of the recipient, sufficient to provide a reasonable basis for the Corporation's determination that the recipient is capable of using the funds for the intended charitable purposes.

§8.2. **Written grant agreement.** A signed, written grant agreement that includes:

§8.2.1. A specification of the charitable purposes for which the Grant is made;
§8.2.2. A commitment by the recipient to use the Grant only for the specified purposes;
§8.2.3. A commitment to repay any portion not used for the specified purposes;
§8.2.4. A commitment to maintain records of receipts and expenditures and to make them available to the Corporation;
§8.2.5. A commitment to provide the Corporation with full and complete annual reports on use of the Grant; and
§8.2.6. A prohibition on use of the Grant for political campaign intervention, lobbying (subject to the lobbying limits applicable to public charities), grants to individuals (other than as authorized under the Grant), or non-charitable purposes.

§8.3. **Reports.** The Corporation shall obtain from the recipient a written report at least annually for the duration of the Grant period and a final report following completion of the Grant.

§8.4. **Form 990 reporting.** The Corporation shall report each Grant subject to Expenditure Responsibility on the Corporation's Form 990 (Schedule I and any other applicable schedule).

§8.5. **Procedures manual.** The Grants Lead shall maintain a procedures manual implementing this §8, which shall be reviewed annually and updated as needed.

## §9. Foreign Grants

§9.1. **Heightened procedures.** Grants to foreign recipients require the heightened procedures set forth in this §9 in addition to the baseline diligence under §6.1 and the approval procedures under §7.

§9.2. **Methods.** The Corporation may make Grants to foreign recipients through:

§9.2.1. Equivalency determination under Rev. Proc. 92-94 (or successor guidance) — supporting that the foreign recipient is the equivalent of a United States 501(c)(3) public charity;
§9.2.2. Expenditure Responsibility under §8; or
§9.2.3. Friends-of organizations or other intermediary arrangements that comply with United States tax and sanctions law.

§9.3. **Sanctions screening.** The Corporation shall screen each foreign recipient and each principal officer or director of the recipient against United States economic sanctions lists, including OFAC's SDN list, prior to approval.

§9.4. **Documentation.** The diligence file for each foreign Grant shall include the basis for the chosen method, the equivalency determination or Expenditure Responsibility documentation, sanctions screening results, and any country-specific risk assessment.

## §10. Grants to Individuals

§10.1. **Heightened procedures.** Grants to individuals (e.g., scholarships, awards, hardship assistance) require the heightened procedures set forth in this §10 in addition to the baseline diligence under §6.1 and the approval procedures under §7.

§10.2. **Selection criteria.** The Corporation shall use objective and nondiscriminatory selection criteria.

§10.3. **Open application process.** Selection shall be made through an open application process, not limited to pre-selected individuals.

§10.4. **Selection committee.** Grants to individuals shall be approved by a selection committee that does not include any person with a family or business relationship with potential recipients.

§10.5. **Section 4945(g) advance approval.** If applicable, the Corporation shall obtain advance approval of its grant procedures under Section 4945(g) of the Code through Form 1023 (at formation) or by separate request.

## §11. Conflict of Interest Coordination

§11.1. Each Director, officer, and key employee of the Corporation shall comply with the Conflict of Interest Policy with respect to all Grants and Grant Recommendations.

§11.2. For each Grant Recommendation, the Grants Lead shall confirm that any required disclosures and recusals under the DUNA Crossover Provisions of the Conflict of Interest Policy have been completed before the Grant Recommendation proceeds to approval.

§11.3. Any Recommendation Proposer who is a Director, officer, or key employee of the Corporation shall recuse themselves from the Corporation's consideration of the related Grant Recommendation in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy. Grants involving Recommendation Proposer recusal must be approved at a regular or special meeting of the Board (and may not be approved by Written Consent), in accordance with §7.3.1, and are subject to additional disclosure in board minutes.

## §12. Documentation; Record-Keeping

§12.1. The Corporation shall maintain, for each Grant:

§12.1.1. The diligence file under §6.6;
§12.1.2. Any written grant agreement;
§12.1.3. Reports received from the recipient;
§12.1.4. Documentation of Board approval, including the form of approval (meeting vote or Written Consent), the date of approval, the approving Directors, and any conflicts disclosure or recusal;
§12.1.5. Documentation of disbursement; and
§12.1.6. For Grant Recommendations, the related Charitable Recommendation Proposal and the disposition communication under §5.9.

§12.2. The records described in this §12 shall be retained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

§12.3. The Corporation shall produce, no less frequently than quarterly, a Grant Register summarizing Grants approved during the quarter, which shall be made available to the full Board and posted (in summary form) on the Corporation's website.

## §13. Reporting and Monitoring

§13.1. **Quarterly reports.** The Grants Lead shall report to the Board, no less frequently than quarterly, on:

§13.1.1. Grants approved during the quarter;
§13.1.2. Grants pending evaluation;
§13.1.3. Grants subject to Expenditure Responsibility, with status of reporting;
§13.1.4. Any compliance issues identified;
§13.1.5. Aggregate grantmaking metrics for the year-to-date.

§13.2. **Annual review.** The Board shall annually review:

§13.2.1. Aggregate grantmaking activity for the prior year;
§13.2.2. The performance and operation of this Policy;
§13.2.3. The thresholds and procedures of this Policy in light of the Corporation's growth and risk profile; and
§13.2.4. Any recommended amendments to this Policy.

## §14. Public Support Test Considerations

The Grants Lead and the Treasurer shall, in coordination with the Corporation's accounting and audit functions, evaluate aggregate grantmaking activity for any implications under the Corporation's public support test under Section 509(a)(1) or 509(a)(2) of the Code, and shall report any concerns to the Board promptly.

## §15. Amendment

This Policy may be amended, in whole or in part, by resolution of the Board, provided that no amendment shall be inconsistent with the Bylaws, the Articles of Incorporation, the Conflict of Interest Policy, or applicable law.

## §16. Effective Date

This Policy is effective on adoption by the Board.
