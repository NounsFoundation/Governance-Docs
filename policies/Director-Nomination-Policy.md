# Director Nomination Policy

## Nouns Foundation

**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

---

## §1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the procedures by which the Board of Directors solicits, receives, vets, and considers candidates for election or appointment as Directors of the Corporation, including the Director Nomination Process by which the Nouns DAO community may submit candidate nominations under the Director Nomination Process provisions of the Bylaws.

## §2. Definitions

For purposes of this Policy, an "**Independent Director**" means a Director who satisfies all four of the following conditions throughout the relevant period:

§2.1. The Director did not receive compensation as an officer or other employee of the Corporation or any related organization. Reasonable compensation paid to the Director for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board does not affect this analysis.

§2.2. The Director did not receive total compensation or other payments from the Corporation and any related organizations as an independent contractor exceeding $10,000 during the year. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses under an accountable plan, and reasonable compensation for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board, are not counted toward the $10,000 threshold.

§2.3. Neither the Director nor any family member of the Director was involved in a transaction with the Corporation, whether directly or indirectly through affiliation with another organization, of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Corporation's Form 990 (Transactions with Interested Persons).

§2.4. Neither the Director nor any family member of the Director was involved in a transaction, whether directly or indirectly, with a taxable or tax-exempt related organization of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Form 990 of that related organization.

For §2.3 and §2.4, Schedule L transactions include excess benefit transactions, loans to or from interested persons, grants or assistance benefiting interested persons, and business transactions involving interested persons. The fact that a Director is a donor to the Corporation, in any amount, does not by itself affect the Director's status as an Independent Director.

This definition is intended to track the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the Internal Revenue Service instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b, as those instructions may be amended from time to time. In the event of any inconsistency between this definition and the Form 990 instructions then in effect, the Form 990 instructions govern for purposes of the Corporation's Form 990 reporting.

## §3. Sources of Director Candidates

Candidates for Director service may be identified through any of the following sources:

§3.1. Search, recruitment, and outreach efforts undertaken by current Directors;

§3.2. Recommendations from current or former Directors;

§3.3. Recommendations from other persons familiar with the Corporation's mission and governance needs;

§3.4. The Director Nomination Process described in §5 of this Policy; and

§3.5. Any other source the Board considers appropriate.

The Board shall consider candidates from each source on the merits and shall not give preference or weight to any candidate based on the source of the recommendation.

The Board shall actively source candidates through the channels described in §3.1 through §3.3 and §3.5 on a continuing basis, and shall not rely exclusively or predominantly on the Director Nomination Process described in §3.4 as the source of new Director candidates. The Board may, in its discretion, decline to advance any candidate from any source where the Board determines the candidate is not appropriate for Director service, whether for reasons of qualification, fit, vetting outcomes, conflicts, or any other reason within the Board's independent fiduciary discretion.

## §4. Vetting Process

§4.1. **Board responsibility.** The Board is responsible for the vetting of all candidates for Director service, regardless of the source of the recommendation. For each candidate or nomination cycle, the Board may, by resolution or motion, designate one or more Directors to coordinate the vetting work (a "**Vetting Coordinator**" for that candidate or cycle); absent designation, the Board Chair shall coordinate the vetting work or shall designate another Director to do so. Designation as a Vetting Coordinator is per-candidate or per-cycle and is not a standing role.

§4.2. **Outside resources.** The Vetting Coordinator may engage outside resources (search firms, background-check vendors, reference-check services) at the Coordinator's discretion within the budget approved by the Board.

§4.3. **Vetting standards.** Each candidate considered by the Board, regardless of the source of the recommendation, shall be subject to the following minimum vetting:

§4.3.1. Background check covering criminal history, civil litigation, regulatory actions, and bankruptcy;

§4.3.2. Sanctions screening, including verification that the candidate does not appear on the Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List maintained by the Office of Foreign Assets Control of the U.S. Department of the Treasury (OFAC) or on any other list of persons with whom the Corporation is prohibited from engaging in transactions under U.S. economic sanctions laws. The Vetting Coordinator may rely on, and is encouraged to leverage, the Corporation's existing identity-verification and sanctions-screening infrastructure (including any infrastructure shared with the Nouns DAO, such as the Persona platform);

§4.3.3. Media check covering public statements, professional reputation, and any reputational risk to the Corporation;

§4.3.4. Reference check with at least three (3) references provided by or identified independently for the candidate;

§4.3.5. Interview by at least two (2) Directors, at least one of whom shall be an Independent Director;

§4.3.6. Conflict-of-interest disclosure consistent with the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy; and

§4.3.7. Confirmation of the candidate's willingness and availability to serve.

§4.4. **Presentation to the Board.** Following completion of the vetting process, the Vetting Coordinator shall present the vetting results and a recommendation to the full Board with respect to each candidate considered. The recommendation may be to elect or appoint the candidate, to defer further consideration, or to decline. All decisions to elect, appoint, defer, or decline a candidate are reserved to the full Board.

§4.5. **Substantive nature of vetting.** The vetting procedures of §4.3 are substantive, not procedural-only. The Vetting Coordinator and the Board are expected, in the regular course, to identify candidates who are not appropriate for Director service and to recommend declining to advance them. The Board's exercise of independent fiduciary discretion under the reservation-of-Board-authority provisions of the Bylaws and §7 of this Policy includes the affirmative responsibility to decline to advance any candidate the Board determines to be inappropriate for Director service, regardless of the source of the recommendation. A pattern in which the Board accepts every candidate from a single source without substantive vetting or evidence of independent judgment is inconsistent with this Policy.


## §5. Director Nomination Process

§5.1. **Mechanism.** The Nouns DAO community may submit candidate nominations to the Board for the Board's consideration through the passage of a Director Nomination Proposal under the standard governance process of the Nouns DAO.

§5.2. **Director Nomination Proposal — required content.** A Director Nomination Proposal shall include:

§5.2.1. The candidate's full legal name;

§5.2.2. A means of contacting the candidate (email address sufficient);

§5.2.3. A statement of the candidate's interest in serving as a Director of the Corporation, prepared or affirmed by the candidate;

§5.2.4. A summary of the candidate's qualifications relevant to Director service, including professional background, prior board experience (if any), and any sector expertise relevant to the Corporation's mission;

§5.2.5. A disclosure of any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest between the candidate and the Corporation, the Nouns DAO, or any related person or organization; and

§5.2.6. The candidate's affirmative consent to being nominated and to participating in the vetting process described in §4.3.

§5.3. **Submission threshold.** A Director Nomination Proposal may be submitted by any person or persons meeting the proposal threshold then in effect under the Nouns DAO governance rules.

§5.4. **Notice of passage.** The Board Chair (or such other Director as the Board may designate) shall be responsible for monitoring Nouns DAO governance for Director Nomination Proposals. The Corporation shall be deemed to have notice of the passage of any such Proposal upon the earlier of:

- actual knowledge of passage by the Board Chair or any other Director; and
- communication of passage by the Nouns DAO Administrators or the proposer to the Corporation's principal mailing address, to the Board Chair, or to the Corporation's website intake channel.

§5.5. **30-Day Consideration.** Within thirty (30) days following the passage of a Director Nomination Proposal, the Board (acting through the Board Chair, the Vetting Coordinator designated under §4.1, or such other Director as the Board may designate for the relevant step) shall:

§5.5.1. Acknowledge receipt of the nomination by communication to the Nouns DAO Administrators or by posting on the Corporation's website (Step 1: intake);

§5.5.2. Confirm the candidate's eligibility under this Policy (Step 2: eligibility check);

§5.5.3. Conduct the vetting procedures described in §4.3 (Steps 3-4: fit assessment and candidate communication);

§5.5.4. Present the vetting results and a recommendation to the full Board with respect to the candidate (Step 5: presentation to Board);

§5.5.5. Following Board action, document the outcome and communicate the disposition back to the Nouns DAO community under §5.6, and cause the Corporation's Secretary to record in the minutes of the next regular or special meeting of the Board:

- that the Director Nomination Proposal was received;
- that the required vetting was conducted;
- the substance of the recommendation presented to the Board; and
- the action taken by the Board with respect to the candidate (Step 6: Board action and documentation).

The Board shall vote on the recommendation in accordance with the Bylaws. No individual Director, including the Board Chair or any Vetting Coordinator, has authority to elect, appoint, defer, or decline any candidate; all such decisions are reserved to the full Board.

§5.6. **Communication of disposition.** Following the Board's action with respect to the candidate, or following the Board's decision (based on the recommendation presented to the Board) not to advance the candidate, the Corporation shall communicate the disposition to the Nouns DAO community through either:

- a posting on the Corporation's website summarizing the disposition; or
- a propdate to the Nouns DAO governance forum.

§5.7. **No further obligation.** Following the disposition described in §5.6, the Corporation has no further obligation under this Policy with respect to the Director Nomination Proposal or the candidate.

## §6. Cap on Seats Subject to the Director Nomination Process

The obligation of consideration set forth in the recognition-of-Nouns-DAO-nominations provisions of the Bylaws shall apply in any calendar year to candidate submissions through the Director Nomination Process for no more than the cap set forth in the cap-on-seats provisions of the Bylaws (currently forty percent (40%) of the seats then constituting the Board, rounded down). Submissions exceeding this cap do not trigger the obligation of consideration. The Board's independent authority to elect or appoint Directors from any source — including from candidates identified through the Director Nomination Process — is not limited by this cap.

## §7. Non-Binding Character

This Policy implements the procedural duty of consideration described in the recognition-of-Nouns-DAO-nominations provisions of the Bylaws. Director nominations submitted under the Director Nomination Process are non-binding inputs that the Board considers in the exercise of its independent fiduciary discretion. Nothing in this Policy creates any power, authority, expectation, or right of any person external to the Board to elect, appoint, remove, suspend, replace, or otherwise compel the seating or unseating of any Director of the Corporation. The Board retains full and final authority over the selection of Directors of the Corporation.

## §8. Documentation; Records

The Corporation shall maintain a record of each Director Nomination Proposal received, the date of passage, the vetting performed, the recommendation presented to the Board, the Board's action, and the date and form of communication of the disposition. The records described in this §8 shall be retained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

## §9. Amendment

This Policy may be amended, in whole or in part, by resolution of the Board, provided that no amendment shall be inconsistent with the Director Nomination Process provisions of the Bylaws.

## §10. Effective Date

This Policy is effective on adoption by the Board.
