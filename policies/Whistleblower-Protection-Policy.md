# Whistleblower Policy
## Nouns Foundation
**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

---

This Policy establishes the procedures by which Directors, officers, contractors, volunteers, grant recipients, and members of the Nouns DAO community may report suspected wrongdoing affecting the Nouns Foundation, Inc. (the "**Corporation**"), and the procedures by which the Corporation receives, investigates, and acts on those reports. The Policy is designed to (a) provide private, protected channels for substantive concerns, (b) prohibit retaliation against any person who reports in good faith, and (c) preserve the public, decentralized character of the Nouns DAO community's ordinary oversight of the Corporation by making the whistleblower channels *additional* and protected rather than exclusive. Inclusion of the Nouns DAO community as a permitted reporter under this Policy does not create any oversight, governance, or appointment authority of the Nouns DAO, the Administrators, or any DAO participant over the Corporation; the Corporation retains exclusive authority over the investigation and disposition of all reports under this Policy.

## §1. Purpose

§1.1. **Compliance and integrity.** The Corporation is a Delaware nonprofit corporation seeking recognition as a public charity under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (the "**Code**"). Maintaining that status, and discharging the Board's fiduciary duties, requires that the Corporation operate with integrity and that suspected wrongdoing be surfaced, evaluated, and addressed promptly.

§1.2. **Channels for reporting.** This Policy establishes private channels through which any covered person may report Reportable Conduct, with anonymity or confidentiality protections to the extent legally permissible.

§1.3. **Protection from retaliation.** This Policy prohibits retaliation against any person who reports suspected wrongdoing in Good Faith, consistent with Section 1107 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 (codified at 18 U.S.C. § 1513(e)) and other applicable law.

§1.4. **Coordination with public DAO oversight.** This Policy does not displace, restrict, or chill the ordinary public discussion, criticism, or oversight of the Corporation that members of the Nouns DAO community may conduct through DAO governance forums, on-chain proposals, or social media. The whistleblower channels in §5 are additional protected channels, available to any person who wishes to use them, and do not replace public community oversight.

§1.5. **Distinction from grievance procedures.** This Policy addresses suspected wrongdoing affecting the Corporation as an institution. It is not a substitute for any human-resources grievance procedure, employment-dispute mechanism, or grant-recipient appeal process the Corporation may from time to time adopt for those distinct purposes.

## §2. Scope

This Policy applies to:

§2.1. Directors of the Corporation;

§2.2. Officers of the Corporation;

§2.3. Key Employees, employees, and interns of the Corporation (when and if any);

§2.4. Independent contractors, consultants, and vendors providing services to the Corporation;

§2.5. Volunteers acting on behalf of the Corporation;

§2.6. Recipients of Grants from the Corporation, with respect to their dealings with the Corporation and their use of Grant funds; and

§2.7. Members of the Nouns DAO community, with respect to their interactions with the Corporation and the Corporation's operations.

Each of the foregoing is a "**Covered Person**." Any Covered Person may submit a report under this Policy.

## §3. Definitions

For purposes of this Policy:

§3.1. "**Whistleblower**" means a Covered Person who reports Reportable Conduct under this Policy.

§3.2. "**Reportable Conduct**" has the meaning set forth in §4.

§3.3. "**Good Faith**" means that, at the time the report is made, the Whistleblower has a reasonable belief that the conduct described is occurring, has occurred, or is about to occur. A report made in Good Faith is protected under this Policy regardless of whether the report is ultimately substantiated; the protection turns on the reasonableness of the Whistleblower's belief at the time of reporting, not on the outcome of the investigation.

§3.4. "**Retaliation**" means any adverse action taken against a Whistleblower, or against a person assisting a Whistleblower or participating in an investigation under this Policy, because of the report or participation. Retaliation includes (without limitation) termination of service or engagement, demotion, exclusion from Board or Committee deliberations, suspension or termination of Grants, public disparagement, threats, harassment, or any other action reasonably likely to deter a reasonable person from reporting or participating.

§3.5. "**Investigator**" means the Board Chair, an Independent Director designated under §7.2, or outside counsel or another independent third party engaged by the Board Chair or by the Board to conduct an investigation under §7.

§3.6. "**Independent Director**" has the meaning set forth in the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy and Director Nomination Policy (tracking the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the IRS instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b).

§3.7. "**Grant**" has the meaning set forth in the Corporation's Grantmaking Policy.

## §4. Reportable Conduct

"**Reportable Conduct**" means any act or omission that a Covered Person reasonably believes constitutes:

§4.1. **Financial misconduct**, including misappropriation of Corporation funds or assets, unauthorized disbursements, falsified financial records, or material misstatements in financial reports filed with any governmental authority (including Form 990) or distributed to the Board, donors, or the public;

§4.2. **Fraud, theft, or embezzlement** involving the Corporation or its funds, property, or information;

§4.3. **Misuse of charitable assets**, including use of Corporation funds, property, or staff time for non-charitable purposes, for the private benefit or inurement of any insider, or in any manner inconsistent with the Corporation's exempt purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code;

§4.4. **Misuse of Foundation funds for non-charitable purposes**, including political campaign intervention, substantial lobbying in violation of Section 501(c)(3) limits, or any expenditure that would jeopardize the Corporation's tax-exempt status;

§4.5. **Violations of law**, including federal, state, or local statutes, regulations, or court orders applicable to the Corporation, its operations, or its grantmaking (including United States economic-sanctions law);

§4.6. **Violations of Corporation policy**, including the Bylaws, the Conflict of Interest Policy, the Grantmaking Policy, the Document Retention Policy, this Policy, or any other policy adopted by the Board;

§4.7. **Undisclosed conflicts of interest**, including any failure by a Director, officer, Key Employee, or Recommendation Proposer to disclose a conflict of interest required to be disclosed under the Conflict of Interest Policy or the Grantmaking Policy;

§4.8. **Harassment or discrimination** directed at any Covered Person in connection with that person's engagement with the Corporation, including conduct prohibited by federal or state anti-discrimination law;

§4.9. **Retaliation** against another Whistleblower or any person assisting an investigation under this Policy; or

§4.10. **Material destruction or alteration of records** that may be the subject of a pending or anticipated investigation, audit, or governmental proceeding.

Reportable Conduct does not include:

- ordinary disagreement with Board decisions made within the Board's discretion;
- the substantive outcome of any Grant decision (declination of a Grant Recommendation is not, by itself, Reportable Conduct); or
- personal grievances of the kind addressed by separate human-resources or contractual processes.

Such matters may be raised through other appropriate channels but are outside the scope of this Policy.

## §5. Reporting Channels

§5.1. **Board Chair.** A Whistleblower may submit a report directly to the Board Chair in writing (including by email) or orally. The Board Chair is the default channel for reports under this Policy and is responsible for the initial review under §7.1.

§5.2. **Any Independent Director.** A Whistleblower may submit a report directly to any Independent Director in writing or orally, particularly where the report concerns the Board Chair or any matter the Whistleblower believes the Board Chair should not be the first to receive. The receiving Independent Director shall promptly forward the report to the Board Chair (where appropriate) or, where appropriate under §7.2, retain it for handling under §7.

§5.3. **Third-party hotline.** When and if the Corporation contracts with a third-party reporting hotline service, reports submitted through that service shall be treated as submissions under this Policy and shall be routed to the Board Chair (or to a designated Independent Director under §7.2 where the Board Chair is the subject of the report).

§5.4. **Anonymous on-chain submission.** The Corporation acknowledges that members of the Nouns DAO community may, from time to time, raise concerns through public on-chain governance proposals, propdates, or other public DAO forums. Such public communications are not prohibited and may be considered by the Corporation, but the Corporation's ability to investigate substantively is limited where the communication does not permit follow-up with the reporter (to clarify facts, request supporting documentation, or refine the scope of inquiry). For substantive investigation, a Whistleblower is encouraged to use one of the channels in §5.1 through §5.3, with anonymity or confidentiality protections under §6 as the Whistleblower prefers.

§5.5. **No exclusivity.** Nothing in this Section requires a Covered Person to use a §5 channel before exercising any right to report suspected wrongdoing to a governmental authority, law-enforcement agency, regulator, or court of competent jurisdiction. The protections of this Policy apply equally to a Whistleblower who reports to the Corporation through this Policy and to a Whistleblower who reports to a governmental authority, provided the report is made in Good Faith.

## §6. Anonymity and Confidentiality

§6.1. **Anonymous reporting.** A Whistleblower may submit a report anonymously through any §5 channel that practically permits anonymity. Anonymous reports will be evaluated and investigated to the extent possible based on the information provided. The Corporation acknowledges that the inability to follow up with an anonymous reporter to clarify facts, request supporting documentation, or test credibility may materially limit the depth of investigation possible and, in some cases, may render substantive investigation impracticable. A Whistleblower who wishes to enable a substantive investigation while protecting their identity from broad disclosure is encouraged to submit a confidential (rather than anonymous) report under §6.2.

§6.2. **Confidential reporting.** A Whistleblower may submit a report on a confidential basis, identifying themselves to the recipient under §5 and requesting that their identity not be disclosed beyond the personnel necessary to conduct the investigation. The recipient shall protect the Whistleblower's identity to the extent legally permissible. The Whistleblower's identity may be disclosed when:

- disclosure is required by law, court order, or governmental subpoena;
- disclosure is necessary to conduct a fair investigation (including disclosure to outside counsel, an Investigator, or, in narrow circumstances, the subject of the report); or
- the Whistleblower consents to disclosure. Where the Corporation reasonably anticipates that a Whistleblower's identity may need to be disclosed to conduct a fair investigation, the Corporation shall, where practicable, notify the Whistleblower in advance and discuss the scope of disclosure.

§6.3. **No promise of absolute confidentiality.** The Corporation does not promise and cannot deliver absolute confidentiality. The protections in this Section are bounded by applicable law and by the practical requirements of conducting a fair investigation.

§6.4. **Protection of subjects.** The identity of any person who is the subject of a report shall, to the extent practicable and consistent with the requirements of investigation, be protected during the pendency of the investigation. Reports under this Policy are not findings; the fact that a report has been made does not constitute evidence that wrongdoing occurred.

## §7. Investigation Procedures

§7.1. **Initial review.** Within ten (10) business days of receipt of a report, the Board Chair shall conduct an initial review to determine:

- whether the report describes Reportable Conduct;
- whether the report contains sufficient information to permit investigation;
- whether any conflict of interest requires reassignment under §7.2; and
- whether the matter warrants engagement of outside counsel or another independent Investigator.

The Board Chair shall document the initial review in writing. For each matter that proceeds to investigation, the Board Chair (or designated Independent Director under §7.2) shall designate the Investigator for the matter, who may be the Board Chair, another Director, an Independent Director, or outside counsel or another independent third party engaged under §7.3. The Investigator is designated on a per-matter basis and is not a standing role.

§7.2. **Reassignment for conflict.** If the Board Chair is the subject of the report, has a conflict of interest with respect to any subject of the report, or is otherwise unable to conduct an impartial review, the report shall be handled by an Independent Director designated by a majority of the Independent Directors then in office. The designated Independent Director shall have all the authorities and responsibilities described elsewhere in §7 with respect to the matter, including designation of the Investigator under §7.1.

§7.3. **Engagement of outside counsel or independent investigator.** The Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2) may engage outside counsel, an outside accounting firm, or another independent third party to conduct or assist with the investigation, within a budget approved by the full Board. Engagement of outside counsel is appropriate for matters involving:

- potential criminal conduct;
- potential violations of federal tax law or jeopardy of the Corporation's exempt status;
- credible allegations against a Director or officer; or
- any matter where the Board Chair (or designated Independent Director under §7.2) determines that an external Investigator is necessary to ensure impartiality or expertise.

§7.4. **Conduct of investigation.** The Investigator shall:

§7.4.1. Gather and review documentary evidence;

§7.4.2. Interview the Whistleblower (where the Whistleblower's identity is known and the Whistleblower is willing), the subject of the report, and any other persons with relevant knowledge;

§7.4.3. Where practicable and consistent with §6, provide the subject of the report with notice of the substance of the allegations and a reasonable opportunity to respond;

§7.4.4. Document the investigation in writing, including the evidence gathered, the analysis performed, and the conclusions reached; and

§7.4.5. Prepare a written investigation report.

§7.5. **Recommended action.** The written investigation report shall recommend action, which may include (without limitation):

- closure of the matter without further action;
- corrective action by the Corporation (policy revision, training, process change);
- disciplinary action against an individual (where the individual is a Director, officer, or contractor of the Corporation);
- referral to law enforcement, a regulator, or other governmental authority;
- restitution or recovery of misappropriated assets; or
- any combination of the foregoing.

§7.6. **Reporting to the Board.** The Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2) shall report the results of every investigation to the Board (or, where the matter involves a sitting Director, to the Independent Directors only). The Board (or the Independent Directors, as applicable) shall determine the action to be taken on each substantiated report. The Board's determination shall be documented in the minutes (with appropriately limited detail to preserve confidentiality where required).

§7.7. **Communication to the Whistleblower.** Where the Whistleblower's identity is known and the Whistleblower can be contacted, the Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2) shall communicate to the Whistleblower:

- acknowledgment of receipt of the report (within five (5) business days of receipt); and
- the conclusion of the investigation, in summary form and to the extent legally permissible.

The Whistleblower is not entitled to a copy of the investigation report or to information about disciplinary or personnel actions taken with respect to other persons.

§7.8. **Timing.** Investigations shall be conducted promptly. Absent unusual circumstances, an investigation should reach a conclusion within ninety (90) days of receipt of the report. Where an investigation requires more time, the Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2) shall report the reason for the delay to the full Board and (where practicable) to the Whistleblower.

## §8. Anti-Retaliation

§8.1. **Prohibition.** The Corporation prohibits Retaliation against any Whistleblower, against any person who participates in good faith in an investigation under this Policy, and against any person who refuses to participate in conduct that the person reasonably believes would constitute Reportable Conduct. This prohibition applies to Retaliation by any Director, officer, employee, contractor, volunteer, or other person acting on behalf of the Corporation.

§8.2. **Covered persons.** All Covered Persons under §2 are protected by this Section, including persons who report through public DAO forums under §5.4 (subject to the limits on substantive investigation described there).

§8.3. **Sarbanes-Oxley §806.** Section 1107 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 (18 U.S.C. § 1513(e)) makes it a federal crime, punishable by fine or imprisonment, for any person knowingly, with the intent to retaliate, to take any action harmful to any person (including interference with the lawful employment or livelihood of any person) for providing to a law-enforcement officer truthful information relating to the commission or possible commission of any federal offense. The whistleblower-retaliation provisions of Sarbanes-Oxley apply to nonprofit organizations (in addition to publicly traded companies) and create criminal liability for retaliation against persons reporting suspected federal crimes — including federal tax violations and federal mail or wire fraud — to law enforcement. The Corporation's anti-retaliation commitment under this Section is independent of and supplements (but does not displace) the protections of Sarbanes-Oxley §1107 and any other applicable federal or state whistleblower-protection law.

§8.4. **Remedies.** A Whistleblower or other protected person who believes they have experienced Retaliation may submit a report describing the suspected Retaliation under §5 (and the Retaliation report shall itself be a report of Reportable Conduct under §4.9). Substantiated Retaliation may result in:

- reversal of the retaliatory action;
- disciplinary action against the person responsible (including, in the case of a Director, recommendation of removal under the Bylaws);
- referral to law enforcement where Section 1107 of Sarbanes-Oxley or other criminal statutes may have been violated; and
- any other remedy the Board determines is appropriate under the circumstances.

§8.5. **Good Faith required.** Anti-retaliation protection extends to reports made in Good Faith as defined in §3.3. Reports made with knowledge of falsity or with reckless disregard for the truth are not protected, and the Corporation may take disciplinary action against any person who knowingly submits a false report.

## §9. Records

§9.1. **Investigation files.** The Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2) shall maintain, for each report, an investigation file containing:

- the report (in original form to the extent practicable);
- the initial-review documentation under §7.1;
- evidence gathered during the investigation;
- the written investigation report;
- documentation of action taken; and
- any communication to the Whistleblower under §7.7.

§9.2. **Retention.** Investigation files shall be retained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy. Investigation files relating to substantiated Reportable Conduct, and any matter that resulted in litigation, governmental inquiry, or referral to law enforcement, shall be retained for the longer of:

- the period required by the Document Retention Policy; and
- seven (7) years from final resolution of the matter.

§9.3. **Restricted access.** Access to investigation files is restricted to the Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2), the Investigator, outside counsel engaged in connection with the matter, and Directors when receiving the investigation report under §7.6. Access by any other person requires the written approval of the Board Chair or, where the Board Chair is the subject of the matter, the Independent Directors.

§9.4. **Document preservation.** Upon receipt of a report, the Board Chair (or the designated Independent Director under §7.2) shall take reasonable steps to ensure that documents and electronic records relevant to the report are preserved and not altered or destroyed pending investigation. Destruction or alteration of relevant records is itself Reportable Conduct under §4.10.

## §10. Annual Reporting

§10.1. **Annual report to the Board.** The Board Chair shall report annually to the full Board on the operation of this Policy. The annual report shall include, for the prior twelve (12) months:

§10.1.1. The total number of reports received under this Policy;

§10.1.2. The disposition of each report (closed at initial review for lack of jurisdiction or insufficient information, investigated and unsubstantiated, investigated and substantiated, pending);

§10.1.3. Categories of conduct reported (in summary form, without identifying individuals);

§10.1.4. Any patterns or trends warranting Board attention;

§10.1.5. The status of any matters referred to law enforcement, regulators, or outside counsel; and

§10.1.6. Recommendations for amendment of this Policy or related policies.

§10.2. **No individual identification.** The annual report shall not identify any individual Whistleblower, subject of a report, or other person, except where the Board Chair (in consultation with outside counsel where appropriate, or, where the Board Chair is the subject of the matter, the designated Independent Director under §7.2) determines that identification is necessary for the Board to discharge its fiduciary duties with respect to a particular matter.

§10.3. **Confidential session.** The annual report shall be received by the Board in a session closed to non-Directors. The minutes of the session shall reflect that the report was received and shall include such summary information as the Board determines is appropriate, but shall not include details that would compromise the confidentiality of any pending or past investigation.

## §11. Amendment

This Policy may be amended, in whole or in part, by resolution of the Board, provided that no amendment shall be inconsistent with the Bylaws, the Articles of Incorporation, the Conflict of Interest Policy, the Document Retention Policy, the Grantmaking Policy, or applicable law (including Section 1107 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002).
