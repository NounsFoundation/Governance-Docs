# Conflict of Interest Policy

## Nouns Foundation

**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

---

## §1. Purpose

§1.1. The Nouns Foundation, Inc. (the "**Corporation**") is a Delaware nonprofit corporation organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and other purposes described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (the "**Code**"). The Corporation's mission is to advance the public good through charitable grants and related activities in four areas:

- **Civic engagement and democratic participation** — supporting the study, development, and deployment of decentralized governance technologies and other initiatives that increase informed public participation in governance and strengthen civic institutions;
- **Public goods, science, and technology** — advancing science and education through grants for open-source infrastructure, freely-licensed public technologies, and scientific research, including research conducted through decentralized or open-collaboration models;
- **Arts and culture** — supporting the creation, presentation, and preservation of artistic and cultural works, including grants to individual artists and arts organizations; and
- **Community welfare** — supporting grassroots, community-based organizations and direct-impact initiatives that promote social welfare and serve communities and people in need.

§1.2. This Conflict of Interest Policy (this "**Policy**") is intended to:

§1.2.1. Protect the Corporation's tax-exempt status and its interests when it is contemplating entering into a transaction or arrangement that might benefit the private interest of an officer, Director, or key employee of the Corporation, or might result in a possible excess benefit transaction under Section 4958 of the Code;

§1.2.2. Establish procedures for the disclosure, evaluation, and management of conflicts of interest in a manner consistent with the duties of loyalty and care owed by Directors and officers under Delaware law;

§1.2.3. Address the recurring scenarios that arise from the Corporation's working relationship with the Nouns DAO (the "**DAO**"), including the consideration of grant recommendations originating in DAO governance; and

§1.2.4. Support the Corporation's reporting and recordkeeping obligations under Form 990 (including Schedule L and Part VI) and the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness procedures under Section 4958 and the regulations thereunder.

§1.3. This Policy is intended to supplement, and not to replace, the duties owed by Directors and officers under the Delaware General Corporation Law (as made applicable through the Delaware Nonprofit Corporation provisions), the Corporation's Certificate of Incorporation, the Corporation's Bylaws, and applicable federal tax law.


## §2. Definitions

For purposes of this Policy:

§2.1. "**Interested Person**" means any Director, officer, or Key Employee of the Corporation, and any other person who, by virtue of position or relationship to the Corporation, is in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the Corporation within the meaning of Section 4958 of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-3.

§2.2. "**Key Employee**" means any employee of the Corporation, other than an officer or Director, who satisfies any of the conditions set forth in the instructions to Form 990, Part VII, for designation as a "key employee" (generally, an employee with reportable compensation exceeding the threshold in those instructions and who has substantial responsibilities or substantial influence over the Corporation's affairs).

§2.3. "**Family Member**" means, with respect to any Interested Person, the person's spouse or domestic partner; the person's parents, children, siblings, grandparents, and grandchildren; the spouses, domestic partners, parents, children, siblings, and grandchildren of any of the foregoing; and any other person residing in the Interested Person's household. The term "Family Member" is intended to be at least as broad as the term "family member" used in the instructions to Form 990, Schedule L.

§2.4. "**Material Financial Interest**" means, with respect to any Interested Person and any transaction, arrangement, or matter, a direct or indirect financial interest of the Interested Person or any Family Member of the Interested Person, including:

§2.4.1. An ownership or investment interest in any entity with which the Corporation has, or is contemplating, a transaction or arrangement;

§2.4.2. A compensation arrangement (including direct or indirect remuneration as well as gifts or favors that are substantial in nature) with the Corporation or with any entity or individual with which the Corporation has, or is contemplating, a transaction or arrangement;

§2.4.3. A potential ownership, investment, or compensation interest in any entity or individual with which the Corporation is negotiating a transaction or arrangement; or

§2.4.4. A grant, payment, or other benefit that the Interested Person or a Family Member would receive, directly or indirectly, as a result of the transaction or arrangement.

A financial interest is not necessarily a Conflict of Interest under §4 below; whether a Conflict of Interest exists is determined under the procedures of §4. For purposes of this definition, a financial interest is "material" if it would reasonably be expected to influence the Interested Person's judgment with respect to the matter, or if a reasonable person in the Interested Person's position would consider the interest material.

§2.5. "**Conflict of Interest**" means a situation in which an Interested Person has a Material Financial Interest in a transaction, arrangement, or matter under consideration by the Corporation, such that the Interested Person's ability to act solely in the best interests of the Corporation with respect to the matter is, or could reasonably be perceived to be, impaired. The term includes any situation that would constitute a "conflict of interest" under the Form 990 instructions or the IRS sample conflict of interest policy in Appendix A of the Form 1023 instructions.

§2.6. "**Independent Director**" means a Director who satisfies all four of the following conditions throughout the relevant period:

§2.6.1. The Director did not receive compensation as an officer or other employee of the Corporation or any related organization. Reasonable compensation paid to the Director for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board does not affect this analysis.

§2.6.2. The Director did not receive total compensation or other payments from the Corporation and any related organizations as an independent contractor exceeding $10,000 during the year. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses under an accountable plan, and reasonable compensation for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board, are not counted toward the $10,000 threshold.

§2.6.3. Neither the Director nor any Family Member of the Director was involved in a transaction with the Corporation, whether directly or indirectly through affiliation with another organization, of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Corporation's Form 990 (Transactions with Interested Persons).

§2.6.4. Neither the Director nor any Family Member of the Director was involved in a transaction, whether directly or indirectly, with a taxable or tax-exempt related organization of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Form 990 of that related organization.

For §2.6.3 and §2.6.4, Schedule L transactions include excess benefit transactions, loans to or from interested persons, grants or assistance benefiting interested persons, and business transactions involving interested persons. The fact that a Director is a donor to the Corporation, in any amount, does not by itself affect the Director's status as an Independent Director. This definition is intended to track the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the Internal Revenue Service instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b, as those instructions may be amended from time to time. In the event of any inconsistency between this definition and the Form 990 instructions then in effect, the Form 990 instructions govern for purposes of the Corporation's Form 990 reporting.


§2.7. "**Related Organization**" means, with respect to the Corporation, any organization that is a "related organization" within the meaning of the instructions to Form 990 and the related-organization rules of Section 4958, including any organization that controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with the Corporation, and any supporting or supported organization with respect to the Corporation. The Nouns DAO is not, by virtue of the recommendation flow described in the Grantmaking Policy or the Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and the DAO, a Related Organization of the Corporation; whether the DAO becomes a Related Organization in any future period is determined by application of the Form 990 and Section 4958 rules to the facts then existing.


§2.8. "**Compensation Arrangement**" means any direct or indirect payment, transfer of value, in-kind benefit, deferred compensation, equity-like interest, expense reimbursement (other than reimbursement under an accountable plan for ordinary and necessary expenses), or other arrangement under which an Interested Person receives, or is entitled to receive, anything of value from the Corporation in exchange for services rendered or to be rendered. The term includes both employment and independent contractor arrangements, and includes compensation paid for service as a Director or officer (whether characterized as a fee, stipend, retainer, or otherwise).

§2.9. "**Recusal**" means, with respect to an Interested Person and a particular matter, the Interested Person's:

§2.9.1. Non-participation in any deliberation, discussion, or vote of the Board (or any committee of the Board) with respect to the matter;

§2.9.2. Physical or virtual absence from any meeting (or portion of a meeting) during which the matter is discussed or voted upon, except to the extent the Interested Person is invited to provide factual information under §4.3.2 and limited to the duration of that information-gathering; and

§2.9.3. Non-participation in any other formal or informal effort to influence the deliberation or decision of the Board with respect to the matter.

§2.10. "**DAO**" means the Nouns DAO, a Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association ("**DUNA**") formed under Wyoming Stat. § 17-32-101 *et seq.* The Nouns DAO is a permissionless, on-chain governance organization in which one Noun non-fungible token ("**Noun**") is auctioned each day, with each Noun conferring one vote in the DAO's governance process. Governance proposals — including treasury disbursements, charitable grants, and other actions of the DAO — are submitted, debated, and approved through that on-chain process.

§2.11. "**Grant Recommendation**" means a non-binding recommendation communicated by the DAO to the Corporation that the Corporation consider making a charitable grant to a specified recipient or class of recipients for a specified charitable purpose. Grant Recommendations are made through the procedure described in the Grantmaking Policy and the Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and the DAO.

§2.12. "**Charitable Recommendation Proposal**" means a governance proposal of the DAO communicating a Grant Recommendation. As of the effective date of this Policy, governance proposals of the DAO are made and passed through the Nouns DAO Governor smart contract identified in the Recommendation Flow provisions of the Grantmaking Policy and the Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and the DAO.

§2.13. "**Recommendation Proposer**" means, with respect to any Grant Recommendation, a person who:

- submitted the Charitable Recommendation Proposal communicating the Grant Recommendation;
- is named as a co-proposer on that Charitable Recommendation Proposal; or
- actively championed passage of the Charitable Recommendation Proposal through public advocacy, sponsored discussion, or organized voting support.

## §3. Duty to Disclose

§3.1. **Affirmative duty.** Each Interested Person has an affirmative duty to disclose to the Board (or, where applicable, to the relevant committee of the Board) the existence of any actual or potential Conflict of Interest, and all material facts relating to any actual or potential Conflict of Interest, of which the Interested Person is or becomes aware.

§3.2. **Timing of disclosure.** Disclosure shall be made:

§3.2.1. At the time the Interested Person is first elected, appointed, or hired into a position that makes the person an Interested Person, by completing the Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form attached as Exhibit A;

§3.2.2. Annually thereafter, by completing a renewed Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form under §7;

§3.2.3. Promptly upon the Interested Person becoming aware of any new or changed Conflict of Interest, and in any event prior to any Board (or committee) deliberation or vote on the matter to which the Conflict of Interest relates; and

§3.2.4. With respect to Grant Recommendations, in accordance with the additional disclosure procedures set forth in §6.

§3.3. **Form of disclosure.** Disclosure shall be made in writing where reasonably practicable, and otherwise on the record at the meeting at which the matter is considered. Verbal disclosures shall be reflected in the minutes of the meeting.

§3.4. **Disclosure by others.** Any Director, officer, or Key Employee who has reason to believe that another Interested Person has a Conflict of Interest that has not been disclosed shall promptly bring the matter to the attention of the Board Chair or, if the Board Chair is the subject of the concern, to the chair of the Audit and Risk Committee or, in the absence of such a committee, to any Independent Director.


## §4. Procedures for Addressing Conflicts

§4.1. **Initial determination of whether a Conflict of Interest exists.**

§4.1.1. After disclosure of the Material Financial Interest and all material facts under §3, and after any discussion with the Interested Person, the Interested Person shall leave the Board (or committee) meeting (or, in the case of a Written Consent process, shall be excluded from the deliberation thereon) while the determination of whether a Conflict of Interest exists is discussed and voted upon.

§4.1.2. The remaining Directors (or committee members), excluding the Interested Person, shall determine by majority vote whether a Conflict of Interest exists.

§4.1.3. The Interested Person may, before leaving the meeting, present a brief explanation of the matter and respond to questions from the remaining Directors. The Interested Person shall not participate in the deliberation or vote on the determination of whether a Conflict of Interest exists.

§4.2. **Procedures for addressing the Conflict of Interest.** If the remaining Directors determine that a Conflict of Interest exists, the following procedures apply:

§4.2.1. The Interested Person shall recuse themselves from the matter in accordance with §2.9.

§4.2.2. The Board Chair (or, if the Board Chair is the Interested Person, such other Director as the remaining Directors may designate) shall, if appropriate, appoint a disinterested person or committee to investigate alternatives to the proposed transaction or arrangement.

§4.2.3. After exercising due diligence, the remaining Directors shall determine whether the Corporation can obtain, with reasonable efforts, a more advantageous transaction or arrangement from a person or entity that would not give rise to a Conflict of Interest.

§4.2.4. If a more advantageous transaction or arrangement is not reasonably possible under circumstances that would not give rise to a Conflict of Interest, the remaining Directors shall determine, by a majority vote of the disinterested Directors, whether the transaction or arrangement is in the Corporation's best interests, is for the Corporation's own benefit, and is fair and reasonable. In making this determination, the remaining Directors shall consider the alternatives, if any, identified under §4.2.3.

§4.2.5. The Board (or committee) shall, by majority vote of the disinterested Directors, decide whether to enter into the transaction or arrangement consistent with the determination under §4.2.4.

§4.3. **Documentation.** The minutes of the Board (or committee) meeting at which a Conflict of Interest is considered shall reflect:

§4.3.1. The names of the persons who disclosed or were otherwise found to have a Material Financial Interest in connection with the actual or potential Conflict of Interest, the nature of the Material Financial Interest, the action taken to determine whether a Conflict of Interest existed, and the Board's or committee's decision as to whether a Conflict of Interest existed;

§4.3.2. The names of the persons who were present for discussions and votes relating to the transaction or arrangement, the content of the discussion (including any alternatives to the proposed transaction or arrangement that were considered), and a record of any votes taken in connection with the proceedings (including the names of any Directors who voted against the action or who abstained); and

§4.3.3. Confirmation that the recused person observed the recusal procedure under §2.9.

§4.4. **Action by Written Consent.** If the matter is being considered by Written Consent under the Bylaws or the Grantmaking Policy, the procedures of this §4 apply with the following adjustments:

- "leave the meeting" means "be excluded from the Written Consent package and from any deliberation conducted asynchronously";
- the Written Consent shall not be circulated to the recused Interested Person; and
- the records described in §4.3 shall be reflected in the next regular Board meeting minutes and in the file copy of the Written Consent.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, certain Grants may not be approved by Written Consent under the mandatory-meeting-consideration provisions of the Grantmaking Policy; in those cases, the matter shall be brought to a regular or special meeting.

§4.5. **Effect of recusal on quorum.** If, by reason of recusals under this Policy, the remaining non-recused Directors would fall below the standard quorum requirement of a majority of Directors then in office, the procedures of this §4 nonetheless proceed as follows: the matter shall be considered by the Board only with the recused Interested Persons absent, and the remaining non-recused Directors shall constitute a quorum for the limited purpose of acting on that matter. This procedure is authorized by Section 144(a)(1) of the Delaware General Corporation Law, which expressly provides that an interested-Director transaction may be approved by a majority of the disinterested Directors even if those disinterested Directors are less than a quorum, and is implemented by the recusal effect on quorum provisions of the Bylaws. The action of a majority of the remaining non-recused Directors is the act of the Board for purposes of that matter, provided that no fewer than two (2) non-recused Directors participate in the deliberation and vote. For purposes of this §4.5, an Interested Person who has been excluded from a Written Consent process under §4.4 is treated the same as an Interested Person physically absent from a meeting under §2.9.

## §5. Compensation Arrangements

§5.1. **General rule.** A voting member of the Board who has a Material Financial Interest, directly or indirectly, in any Compensation Arrangement of the Corporation (including the member's own Compensation Arrangement) is precluded from voting on, and from participating in any deliberation regarding, the Compensation Arrangement.

§5.2. **Specific applications.** The general rule under §5.1 applies to:

§5.2.1. The Compensation Arrangement of the voting member;

§5.2.2. The Compensation Arrangement of any Family Member of the voting member; and

§5.2.3. The Compensation Arrangement of any other person with respect to whom the voting member has a Material Financial Interest.

§5.3. **Independent body review.** Compensation Arrangements with any Interested Person shall be reviewed and approved by a body of the Board composed entirely of Directors who:

- do not have a Conflict of Interest with respect to the Compensation Arrangement; and
- satisfy the "independent" requirements applicable to the rebuttable presumption procedure under Section 4958 of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6 (which generally requires that the approving body be composed of individuals who are not in a conflict-of-interest position with respect to the compensation arrangement and who do not have a material financial interest in the arrangement).

§5.4. **Rebuttable presumption procedure.** Compensation Arrangements with any Interested Person shall be approved in accordance with the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness procedure described in the Corporation's Compensation Review Process. That procedure requires:

- approval by an independent body free of conflicts;
- reliance on appropriate comparability data demonstrating the reasonableness of the compensation; and
- contemporaneous documentation of the basis for the determination,

all consistent with Section 4958 of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6. Section 4958 of the Code imposes excise taxes on "excess benefit transactions" between an applicable tax-exempt organization and any person who is in a position to exercise substantial influence over the organization's affairs, and on the organization managers who knowingly participate in such transactions; satisfying the rebuttable presumption procedure shifts the burden to the IRS to prove that the compensation is unreasonable.

§5.5. **Cross-reference to DUNA crossover provisions.** §6.9 addresses the application of this §5 to Director Compensation Arrangements.


## §6. DUNA-Crossover Provisions

§6.1. **Background and purpose.**

§6.1.1. *The Nouns DAO.* The Nouns DAO (the "**DAO**," as defined in §2.10) is a permissionless on-chain governance organization formed as a Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association ("**DUNA**"). The DAO operates by auctioning one Noun non-fungible token (each, a "**Noun**") each day; each Noun confers one vote in the DAO's governance process. Governance proposals — including disbursements from the DAO's treasury — are made, debated, and passed through that on-chain process. The DAO has historically supported charitable and public-goods initiatives through grants funded from its treasury.

§6.1.2. *Formation context.* The Corporation is being formed at the initiative of the DAO to enable charitable grantmaking through a structure qualifying for tax-exempt status under §501(c)(3) of the Code. The Corporation and the DAO are separate legal entities. The DAO does not own the Corporation, does not control or appoint the Corporation's Directors, and has no authority over the Corporation's grantmaking decisions. The Corporation is governed by an independent self-perpetuating Board of Directors as set forth in the Bylaws.

§6.1.3. *Operational relationship.* The Corporation may receive non-binding Grant Recommendations from the DAO under the procedure described in the Grantmaking Policy and the Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and the DAO. The Board considers each such Recommendation in the exercise of its independent fiduciary discretion and retains ultimate authority over every grant decision under the Grantmaking Policy. The Corporation may also use Nouns brand assets and iconography that the Nouns project has dedicated to the public domain under the Creative Commons Zero (CC0) license.

§6.1.4. *Why this Section exists.* Because the Corporation and the DAO operate in close coordination on charitable grantmaking, recurring scenarios arise in which a Director, officer, or Key Employee of the Corporation may also hold Nouns, participate in the DAO's governance, or have personally proposed or championed a Grant Recommendation that comes before the Corporation for consideration. This §6 establishes how those situations are disclosed and managed under this Policy. The provisions of this §6 supplement, and do not replace, the standard procedures of §3 through §5.

§6.2. **Definitions.** For purposes of this §6:

§6.2.1. "**Nouns Holder**" means any person who holds one or more Nouns NFTs at any time during the relevant period, or who exercises voting power as a delegate of Nouns held by another person.

§6.2.2. "**Material DAO Participation**" means, with respect to a Director, officer, or Key Employee, any of the following:

- holding or being delegated voting power equal to or exceeding ten percent (10%) of the minimum quorum required to pass a Nouns DAO governance proposal as of the date of measurement;
- serving in any formal DAO governance role, including but not limited to Administrator;
- acting as a public Nouns delegate maintaining a delegate platform; or
- regularly participating in the drafting, championing, or active promotion of DAO governance proposals.


§6.3. **Standing disclosure of Nouns affiliation.** Each Director, officer, and Key Employee of the Corporation shall, on first taking office and annually thereafter (and within thirty (30) days of any material change), disclose to the Board:

§6.3.1. Whether the person is a Nouns Holder, and if so, the approximate voting power held or delegated as of the date of disclosure, expressed both as:

- the approximate number of Nouns held or delegated; and
- the approximate percentage of the minimum quorum required to pass a Nouns DAO governance proposal as of the date of disclosure;

§6.3.2. Whether the person engages in Material DAO Participation, and if so, the nature of that participation; and

§6.3.3. Any material change in the foregoing during the prior year.

The disclosures required under this §6.3 shall be recorded by the Corporation and made available to the full Board.


§6.4. **Treatment of Nouns holding (without more).** Holding Nouns or engaging in Material DAO Participation alone — in the absence of any of the additional factors set forth in §6.5 — does not, by itself, require recusal of a Director, officer, or Key Employee from the Corporation's consideration of any Grant Recommendation. The Board, in its independent fiduciary discretion, may consider Nouns affiliation as one factor among others in evaluating any potential conflict but is not required to treat Nouns affiliation alone as disqualifying.

§6.5. **Required recusal — recommendation-specific conflicts.** A Director, officer, or Key Employee of the Corporation shall recuse themselves from the Corporation's consideration of a specific Grant Recommendation if any of the following applies with respect to that Grant Recommendation:

§6.5.1. The person is a Recommendation Proposer with respect to the Grant Recommendation;

§6.5.2. The person has a direct or indirect financial interest in the proposed recipient (including any beneficial ownership, anticipated employment, anticipated grant flow back to the person or a related entity, or any other economic interest);

§6.5.3. The person has a family relationship with the proposed recipient, the recipient's principal officers or directors, or any Recommendation Proposer (where "family" includes the relationships set forth in the definition of Family Member in §2.3);

§6.5.4. The person has a business relationship with the proposed recipient (including service as an officer, director, employee, or material consultant of the recipient or a related entity within the prior twenty-four (24) months); or

§6.5.5. The person determines, in good-faith judgment, that the person cannot impartially consider the Grant Recommendation under this Policy.

Recusal under this §6.5 shall be effected in accordance with §2.9 and shall be recorded in the minutes of the relevant Board (or committee) meeting in accordance with §6.7.

§6.6. **Voluntary DAO-side abstention — acknowledgment without requirement.** The Corporation does not impose any requirement on Directors, officers, or Key Employees with respect to voting, abstention, or other participation in DAO governance proposals, including Charitable Recommendation Proposals communicating Grant Recommendations to the Corporation. The DAO is a separate legal entity governed by its own bylaws, and the Corporation neither has nor seeks any authority over the conduct of Directors, officers, or Key Employees in their personal capacity as Nouns Holders.

A Director, officer, or Key Employee may, as a matter of personal best practice, choose to abstain from voting in DAO governance on a Charitable Recommendation Proposal that the person reasonably anticipates will come before the Corporation for consideration. Such voluntary abstention is acknowledged but not required by this Policy.

§6.7. **Documentation in board records.** Following each Board (or committee) consideration of a Grant Recommendation, the Corporation's Secretary shall record in the minutes:

§6.7.1. The disclosures made under §6.3 that are relevant to the Grant Recommendation;

§6.7.2. Any recusals under §6.5, including the basis for recusal;

§6.7.3. Confirmation that the consideration was conducted by Directors free of recusal-triggering conflicts; and

§6.7.4. The disposition of the Grant Recommendation.

§6.8. **Schedule L disclosure.** The Corporation shall evaluate each Grant Recommendation, recusal, and grant disposition for Form 990 Schedule L disclosure obligations in accordance with the Schedule L instructions. Disclosures under this §6.8 are in addition to, and do not replace, the disclosure requirements of §6.3 or any other provision of this Policy.

§6.9. **Director compensation.** Any Compensation Arrangement for Director service shall be evaluated, approved, and documented under the rebuttable presumption procedure described in §5 and the Compensation Review Process — the safe harbor under Section 4958 of the Code that, when followed, causes the IRS to presume the compensation is reasonable.

§6.10. **Coordination with other policies.** This §6 supplements the other provisions of this Policy and shall be read in coordination with:

- the Grantmaking Policy, which describes the operational flow for Grant Recommendation consideration;
- the Memorandum of Understanding between the Corporation and the DAO, which memorializes the recommendation-flow framework between the entities; and
- the Compensation Review Process.

In the event of any inconsistency between this §6 and the other provisions of this Policy with respect to a Grant Recommendation, this §6 governs.

## §7. Annual Statements

§7.1. **Required signatories.** Each Director, officer, and Key Employee of the Corporation shall annually sign the Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form attached as Exhibit A (or a substantially similar form approved by the Board).

§7.2. **Required affirmations.** Each signed Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form shall affirm that the signing person:

§7.2.1. Has received a copy of this Policy;

§7.2.2. Has read and understands this Policy;

§7.2.3. Agrees to comply with this Policy;

§7.2.4. Understands that the Corporation is a public charity and, in order to maintain its federal tax exemption, must engage primarily in activities that accomplish one or more of its tax-exempt purposes; and

§7.2.5. Has disclosed all known actual and potential Conflicts of Interest, all known Material Financial Interests, and all required disclosures under §6.3 (Standing Disclosure of Nouns Affiliation) as of the date of signing, and acknowledges the ongoing duty to disclose under §3.

§7.3. **Timing.** The initial Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form shall be signed by each Interested Person within thirty (30) days following election, appointment, or hire. Renewed forms shall be signed annually thereafter, in connection with the Board's annual review under §8 or otherwise on a schedule established by the Board Chair or Secretary.

§7.4. **Recordkeeping.** Signed Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Forms shall be retained by the Corporation in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

## §8. Periodic Reviews

§8.1. **Annual review.** To ensure that the Corporation operates in a manner consistent with its charitable purposes and does not engage in activities that could jeopardize its tax-exempt status, the Board shall annually conduct a review covering, at a minimum:

§8.1.1. Whether Compensation Arrangements and benefits are reasonable, are based on competent survey or comparability information, and are the result of arm's-length bargaining, consistent with Section 4958 of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6;

§8.1.2. Whether partnerships, joint ventures, contracts, and arrangements with management organizations conform to the Corporation's written policies, are properly recorded, reflect reasonable investment or payments for goods and services, further the Corporation's charitable purposes, and do not result in inurement, impermissible private benefit, or in an excess benefit transaction;

§8.1.3. Whether grants and recommendation flow from the DAO have been considered consistently with the Grantmaking Policy and the procedures of §6 of this Policy;

§8.1.4. Compliance with the disclosure and recusal requirements of this Policy by Directors, officers, and Key Employees during the prior year; and

§8.1.5. Any actual or potential Conflicts of Interest that arose during the prior year, the procedures applied, and the dispositions reached.

§8.2. **Outside experts.** The Board may engage the assistance of outside experts under §11 to support the periodic review.

§8.3. **Reporting.** The results of the annual review shall be summarized in the minutes of the Board meeting at which the review is conducted, together with any actions taken or recommended in response.

§8.4. **Cross-document reference verification.** As part of each periodic review, the Board shall confirm that the cross-references in this Policy to other Corporation governance documents (the Bylaws, the Grantmaking Policy, the Compensation Review Process, the Director Nomination Policy, the Document Retention Policy, the Whistleblower Policy, and any other policy adopted by the Board) accurately identify the relevant provisions of those documents as then in effect, and shall update any cross-references that have become inaccurate due to amendment.

## §9. Records

The minutes of any Board (or committee) meeting at which a Conflict of Interest, Material Financial Interest, recusal, or matter under §6 is discussed shall include:

§9.1. The names of the persons who disclosed or were otherwise found to have a Material Financial Interest, the nature of the Material Financial Interest, the action taken to determine whether a Conflict of Interest existed, and the Board's or committee's decision as to whether a Conflict of Interest existed;

§9.2. The names of the persons who were present for discussions and votes relating to the transaction or arrangement, the content of the discussion (including any alternatives to the proposed transaction or arrangement that were considered), and a record of any votes taken in connection with the proceedings (including the names of any Directors who voted against the action or who abstained);

§9.3. For matters under §6, the additional information required by §6.7;

§9.4. For Compensation Arrangements, the additional information required by §5 and the Compensation Review Process; and

§9.5. Confirmation that any required recusals were observed in accordance with §2.9.

The records described in this §9 shall be retained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

## §10. Violations

§10.1. **Investigation.** If the Board (or any committee designated by the Board) has reasonable cause to believe an Interested Person has failed to disclose an actual or potential Conflict of Interest, has failed to recuse themselves where required, or has otherwise violated this Policy, the Board (or designated committee) shall inform the Interested Person of the basis for such belief and afford the Interested Person an opportunity to explain the alleged failure to disclose or other violation.

§10.2. **Determination and corrective action.** If, after hearing the Interested Person's response and after making such further investigation as may be warranted in the circumstances, the Board (or designated committee) determines that the Interested Person has in fact failed to disclose an actual or potential Conflict of Interest, has failed to recuse themselves where required, or has otherwise violated this Policy, the Board shall take appropriate disciplinary and corrective action. Corrective action may include, without limitation:

§10.2.1. Requiring the Interested Person to recuse themselves prospectively from the matter and from related matters;

§10.2.2. Voiding, modifying, or rescinding any transaction or arrangement entered into in violation of this Policy, to the extent permitted by law;

§10.2.3. Requiring the Interested Person to make restitution to the Corporation;

§10.2.4. Removing the Interested Person from any committee or office held by the Interested Person, to the extent within the Board's authority under the Bylaws;

§10.2.5. Recommending the removal of the Interested Person from the Board, to the extent the removal procedures of the Bylaws and applicable law permit; and

§10.2.6. Reporting the violation to the Internal Revenue Service, state regulators, or other authorities, where required by law or warranted in the Board's judgment.

§10.3. **No exclusivity.** The corrective actions in this §10 are in addition to, and not in lieu of, any rights or remedies available to the Corporation under applicable law.

## §11. Use of Outside Experts

§11.1. When conducting the periodic reviews under §8, evaluating particular Compensation Arrangements under §5, considering complex Conflicts of Interest under §4, or otherwise implementing this Policy, the Corporation may engage outside advisors — including legal counsel, accountants, compensation consultants, valuation experts, governance consultants, and other professionals — to assist the Board in carrying out its responsibilities under this Policy.

§11.2. The use of outside experts does not relieve the Board of its responsibility for ensuring that periodic reviews are conducted, that Compensation Arrangements are reasonable, that Conflicts of Interest are appropriately addressed, and that this Policy is implemented faithfully.

§11.3. Reasonable compensation paid to outside experts may be reimbursed or paid by the Corporation as an ordinary and necessary expense of the Corporation's operations, subject to the Bylaws and any budget approved by the Board.

## §12. Amendment

§12.1. This Policy may be amended, in whole or in part, by resolution of the Board, provided that no amendment shall be inconsistent with the Bylaws, the Certificate of Incorporation, applicable Delaware law, applicable federal tax law, or the rebuttable presumption procedure under Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6.

§12.2. Amendments to the definition of Independent Director in §2.6 should be coordinated with corresponding amendments to the Director Nomination Policy and the Grantmaking Policy, in which the same definition appears.

§12.3. Each amendment shall be reflected in the Amendment History table at the head of this Policy and shall be effective as of the date stated in the resolution adopting the amendment (or, if no date is stated, as of the date of adoption).
