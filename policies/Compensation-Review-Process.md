# Compensation Review Process
## Nouns Foundation
**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

---

## §1. Purpose

This Process establishes the procedure by which the Board of Directors of the Nouns Foundation, Inc. (the "**Corporation**") reviews and approves compensation arrangements with Disqualified Persons. The Process is designed to:

§1.1. satisfy the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness under Section 4958(a) of the Internal Revenue Code (the "**Code**") and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6, so that any compensation arrangement reviewed under this Process is presumed reasonable and the burden of proof shifts to the Internal Revenue Service to demonstrate otherwise;

§1.2. document compensation decisions in a manner that supports the Corporation's reporting obligations under Form 990, Part VII (Compensation of Officers, Directors, Trustees, Key Employees, and Highest Compensated Employees) and Schedule J (Compensation Information);

§1.3. protect the Corporation, its Directors, and its officers from the excise taxes and other consequences associated with excess benefit transactions under Section 4958 of the Code; and

§1.4. coordinate compensation review with the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy and other governance documents.

This Process is not a substitute for the substantive judgment of the Board concerning the appropriateness of any particular compensation arrangement; it is the procedural framework within which that substantive judgment is exercised and recorded.

## §2. Scope

§2.1. **Covered arrangements.** This Process applies to every Compensation Arrangement between the Corporation and any Disqualified Person, including:

§2.1.1. Compensation paid to Directors for service as a Director;

§2.1.2. Compensation paid to officers (including the Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, Grants Lead, or any other officer);

§2.1.3. Compensation paid to Key Employees and other Disqualified Persons; and

§2.1.4. Independent contractor arrangements with Disqualified Persons.

§2.2. **Coordination with Conflict of Interest Policy.** Any Director, officer, or Key Employee with a conflict of interest in a Compensation Arrangement under review is an Interested Person under the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy and shall comply with the disclosure and recusal requirements of that Policy in addition to the procedures set forth in this Process. The standards in §10 of this Process supplement, and do not replace, the Conflict of Interest Policy.

§2.3. **Exclusions.** This Process does not apply to:

§2.3.1. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in the conduct of Corporation business and substantiated under an accountable plan within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 1.62-2; or

§2.3.2. Routine arrangements with vendors and contractors who are not Disqualified Persons (which are governed by ordinary procurement and approval procedures established by the Board or by management).

## §3. Definitions

For purposes of this Process:

§3.1. "**Disqualified Person**" means, with respect to any transaction, any person who was, at any time during the five-year period ending on the date of the transaction, in a position to exercise substantial influence over the affairs of the Corporation, within the meaning of Section 4958(f)(1) of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-3. Persons in a position to exercise substantial influence include, without limitation, voting members of the Board, officers of the Corporation, and any Key Employee. The term also includes:

- family members of any such person (within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-3(b)(1)); and
- entities in which any such person (or family member) holds more than a 35% controlling interest.

Whether a person is a Disqualified Person in any particular case is a facts-and-circumstances determination informed by the factors set forth in Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-3(e).

§3.2. "**Compensation Arrangement**" means any arrangement under which the Corporation provides economic benefit to a Disqualified Person in exchange for the performance of services, including:

§3.2.1. Salary, wages, and bonuses (cash or non-cash);

§3.2.2. Deferred compensation, retirement contributions, and severance arrangements;

§3.2.3. Fringe benefits (health and welfare benefits, insurance premiums, perquisites);

§3.2.4. Equity-equivalent or token-based compensation, if any;

§3.2.5. Expense reimbursements that are not made under an accountable plan; and

§3.2.6. Any other transfer of value from the Corporation that is consideration for services rendered by the Disqualified Person.

§3.3. "**Independent Body**" means the body constituted under §5 of this Process — either the full Board acting through its Independent Directors, or a committee of Independent Directors authorized by the Board — that reviews and approves a Compensation Arrangement under this Process.

§3.4. "**Independent Director**" means a Director who satisfies all four of the following conditions throughout the relevant period:

§3.4.1. The Director did not receive compensation as an officer or other employee of the Corporation or any related organization. Reasonable compensation paid to the Director for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board does not affect this analysis.

§3.4.2. The Director did not receive total compensation or other payments from the Corporation and any related organizations as an independent contractor exceeding $10,000 during the year. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses under an accountable plan, and reasonable compensation for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board, are not counted toward the $10,000 threshold.

§3.4.3. Neither the Director nor any family member of the Director was involved in a transaction with the Corporation, whether directly or indirectly through affiliation with another organization, of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Corporation's Form 990 (Transactions with Interested Persons).

§3.4.4. Neither the Director nor any family member of the Director was involved in a transaction, whether directly or indirectly, with a taxable or tax-exempt related organization of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Form 990 of that related organization.

For §3.4.3 and §3.4.4, Schedule L transactions include excess benefit transactions, loans to or from interested persons, grants or assistance benefiting interested persons, and business transactions involving interested persons. The fact that a Director is a donor to the Corporation, in any amount, does not by itself affect the Director's status as an Independent Director. This definition is intended to track the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the Internal Revenue Service instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b, as those instructions may be amended from time to time. In the event of any inconsistency between this definition and the Form 990 instructions then in effect, the Form 990 instructions govern for purposes of the Corporation's Form 990 reporting.


§3.5. "**Comparability Data**" means data on compensation paid by similarly situated organizations (taxable or tax-exempt) for functionally comparable positions, of the kind described in Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6(c)(2), including the sources enumerated in §6 of this Process.

§3.6. "**Excess Benefit Transaction**" means a transaction in which an economic benefit is provided by the Corporation, directly or indirectly, to or for the use of any Disqualified Person, where the value of the economic benefit exceeds the value of the consideration (including the performance of services) received for providing such benefit, within the meaning of Section 4958(c)(1) of the Code.

§3.7. "**Key Employee**" has the meaning set forth in the Form 990 instructions (generally, an employee of the Corporation, other than an officer, director, or trustee, who satisfies all three of the $150,000 reportable compensation test, the responsibility test, and the top-20 test as defined in those instructions).

§3.8. "**Rebuttable Presumption**" means the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness available under Section 4958(a) of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6, which arises with respect to a Compensation Arrangement if the three procedural requirements set forth in §4 of this Process are satisfied.

§3.9. "**Interested Person**" has the meaning set forth in the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy.

## §4. The Three-Prong Rebuttable Presumption Procedure

§4.1. **Statutory framework.** Under Section 4958(a) of the Code and Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6, a Compensation Arrangement between the Corporation and a Disqualified Person is presumed to be reasonable — and therefore not an Excess Benefit Transaction — if all three of the following requirements are satisfied. When the Rebuttable Presumption applies, the burden of proving that the arrangement is unreasonable shifts to the Internal Revenue Service, which must develop sufficient contrary evidence to overcome the presumption.

The three requirements are:

§4.1.1. **Independent Body Approval.** The Compensation Arrangement is approved in advance by an Independent Body composed entirely of individuals who do not have a conflict of interest with respect to the arrangement (see §5);

§4.1.2. **Comparability Data.** The Independent Body obtains and relies upon appropriate comparability data prior to making its determination (see §6); and

§4.1.3. **Contemporaneous Documentation.** The Independent Body adequately and contemporaneously documents the basis for its determination (see §7).

§4.2. **Failure of any prong.** Failure to satisfy any of the three requirements means that the Rebuttable Presumption does not apply. In that event, the Compensation Arrangement is not automatically deemed unreasonable, but the Corporation bears the burden of establishing reasonableness on the underlying facts and circumstances. The Corporation shall make best efforts to satisfy all three requirements with respect to every Compensation Arrangement covered by this Process.

§4.3. **Substantive standard preserved.** The Rebuttable Presumption is procedural. The Independent Body remains responsible for exercising substantive judgment that any Compensation Arrangement it approves is, in fact, reasonable in amount and appropriate in light of the services to be rendered.

## §5. Independent Body

§5.1. **Composition.** The Independent Body shall consist exclusively of Directors who satisfy each of the following:

§5.1.1. The Director is an Independent Director within the meaning of §3.4 (Form 990 four-prong test);

§5.1.2. The Director does not have, and is not subject to, any conflict of interest with respect to the Compensation Arrangement under review (including any conflict triggering recusal under the Conflict of Interest Policy);

§5.1.3. The Director is not the Disqualified Person whose compensation is under review, and is not a family member of, or in a business relationship with, that Disqualified Person; and

§5.1.4. The Director has not received compensation from the Corporation in any capacity (whether as employee, officer, or independent contractor) at any time during the prior twelve (12) months. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses under an accountable plan does not count for this purpose.

§5.2. **Minimum size.** The Independent Body shall consist of not fewer than three (3) Directors satisfying §5.1. Where fewer than three Directors qualify with respect to a particular Compensation Arrangement, the Board shall consider:

- deferring the arrangement until a qualified Independent Body can be constituted;
- appointing additional Independent Directors to enable a qualified Independent Body; or
- seeking compensation review by a qualified outside person under Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6(c)(1)(ii) (independent compensation consultant or counsel) and presenting the resulting recommendation to the Independent Body for approval.


§5.3. **Constitution.** The Independent Body may be:

- the full Board acting in a session limited to Directors satisfying §5.1;
- a standing committee of the Board composed of Directors satisfying §5.1; or
- an ad hoc committee constituted by Board resolution for the specific Compensation Arrangement under review.

§5.4. **Procedure.** The Independent Body shall:

§5.4.1. Meet in a session separate from the conduct of other Board business, with attendance limited to members of the Independent Body and any non-conflicted advisors invited to assist;

§5.4.2. Receive and consider Comparability Data prepared in accordance with §6, and any other materials the Independent Body considers relevant;

§5.4.3. Deliberate the proposed Compensation Arrangement on the merits, including the reasonableness of total compensation in light of the services to be rendered and the Comparability Data;

§5.4.4. Vote on the proposed Compensation Arrangement; and

§5.4.5. Cause the basis for its determination to be documented in accordance with §7.

§5.5. **Approval threshold.** Approval of a Compensation Arrangement by the Independent Body requires the affirmative vote of a majority of the members of the Independent Body present and voting at a meeting at which a quorum (a majority of the Independent Body) is present.

§5.6. **Minutes.** Minutes of each Independent Body session shall be prepared by the Secretary or by a member of the Independent Body designated for the purpose, and shall be retained in accordance with §11.

## §6. Comparability Data

§6.1. **Required sources.** The Independent Body shall obtain and consider Comparability Data drawn from at least the following sources, to the extent available and relevant to the Compensation Arrangement under review:

§6.1.1. **Form 990 filings of comparable organizations.** Compensation reported on Forms 990 (and Forms 990-EZ and 990-PF, as applicable) of organizations comparable to the Corporation in mission, asset size, geographic scope, and operational complexity. Comparable organizations should be selected with attention to the Corporation's distinctive profile (substantial onchain treasury, relationship to a sponsoring DAO, grantmaking model);

§6.1.2. **Compensation surveys.** Published compensation surveys covering the relevant role, including (without limitation) surveys published by the Council on Foundations, BoardSource, the Nonprofit Times, Candid (formerly GuideStar), and any other reputable source covering nonprofit or foundation compensation; and

§6.1.3. **Independent compensation studies.** For executive Compensation Arrangements with annual total compensation reasonably expected to exceed $150,000, an independent compensation study prepared by a qualified outside compensation consultant. The Independent Body may waive this requirement only by recorded resolution stating the basis for the waiver (e.g., that adequate Comparability Data is available from the other sources at no incremental cost to the Corporation).

§6.2. **Selection of comparables.** Selection of comparable organizations should reflect the Corporation's distinctive profile, including its treasury size and operational footprint as well as its grantmaking volume. The Independent Body shall document the rationale for the comparable set selected.


§6.3. **Functionally comparable positions.** Comparability Data shall reflect compensation paid for "functionally comparable" positions, within the meaning of Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6(c)(2). The Independent Body shall document its assessment of functional comparability where the role under review differs materially from a standard reference role (e.g., where the Grants Lead's responsibilities at the Corporation span functions that, at a larger organization, would be allocated to multiple positions).

§6.4. **Documentation requirement.** Copies of all Comparability Data relied upon by the Independent Body — including the Forms 990 reviewed, the survey excerpts considered, and any consultant report obtained — shall be retained in the Corporation's records in accordance with the Document Retention Policy and made available for examination by the Internal Revenue Service or the Corporation's auditors.

§6.5. **Currency of data.** Comparability Data should generally reflect compensation paid within the most recent three (3) years. The Independent Body may rely on older data only where current data is not reasonably available, and shall document the basis for doing so.

## §7. Contemporaneous Documentation

§7.1. **Timing.** Documentation of the basis for the Independent Body's determination shall be prepared and approved by the Independent Body before the later of:

- the next regular or special meeting of the Independent Body following the meeting at which the Compensation Arrangement was approved; or
- sixty (60) days after the final action of the Independent Body with respect to the Compensation Arrangement,

in accordance with Treas. Reg. § 53.4958-6(c)(3)(ii).

§7.2. **Required content.** The contemporaneous documentation shall include, at minimum:

§7.2.1. The terms of the Compensation Arrangement approved (including all components of compensation, the duration of the arrangement, and any conditions or contingencies);

§7.2.2. The date the arrangement was approved;

§7.2.3. The members of the Independent Body who were present during deliberation and the members who voted on the arrangement;

§7.2.4. The Comparability Data obtained and relied upon, with a description of how the data was obtained and considered;

§7.2.5. The basis for the Independent Body's determination that the compensation is reasonable in light of the Comparability Data and the services to be rendered;

§7.2.6. Any actions taken with respect to consideration of the arrangement by any member of the Independent Body who had a conflict of interest with respect to the arrangement (including the disclosure of the conflict and the recusal procedures followed); and

§7.2.7. Any other information the Independent Body considered material to its determination.

§7.3. **Form.** The contemporaneous documentation may take the form of meeting minutes, a written resolution of the Independent Body, or a separate compensation memorandum approved by the Independent Body. In any form, the documentation shall be sufficient to demonstrate, on its face, the satisfaction of each of the three prongs in §4.1 and the substantive considerations underlying the Independent Body's determination of reasonableness.

§7.4. **Approval.** The contemporaneous documentation shall be approved by the Independent Body within the time period set forth in §7.1. Approval may be effected by adoption at a subsequent meeting of the Independent Body, or by written consent of the Independent Body if permitted by the Bylaws.

## §8. Initial and Annual Review

§8.1. **Initial review.** Every new Compensation Arrangement covered by this Process shall be reviewed and approved under this Process before the arrangement takes effect or any compensation is paid. Where exigent circumstances make advance review impracticable (e.g., a successor officer must be installed before the next available Independent Body meeting), the Board shall constitute the Independent Body on an expedited basis and shall not authorize compensation to be paid before the Independent Body has acted.

§8.2. **Annual review of executive compensation.** Each Compensation Arrangement with an officer of the Corporation (and any other executive Compensation Arrangement designated by the Board) shall be reviewed by the Independent Body at least annually under this Process, regardless of whether the terms of the arrangement have changed. Annual review may confirm the existing arrangement, modify it, or terminate it.

§8.3. **Triennial review of other arrangements.** Other Compensation Arrangements (including Director compensation, if any, and compensation paid to other Disqualified Persons who are not officers) shall be reviewed by the Independent Body at least once every three (3) years under this Process, and in any case promptly upon any material change in the terms of the arrangement, the duties of the position, or the relationship between the Corporation and the Disqualified Person.

§8.4. **Material change.** A "material change" includes, without limitation, a change in base compensation greater than ten percent (10%), the addition or removal of any compensation component (e.g., introduction of a bonus or deferred compensation arrangement), a change in the scope or nature of the services to be rendered, or any extension or renewal of the arrangement beyond its original term.

## §9. Specific Procedures by Role

§9.1. **Directors.** Directors shall serve without compensation other than reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in the conduct of Corporation business and substantiated under an accountable plan. Reimbursement of expenses under an accountable plan is not a Compensation Arrangement and is not subject to this Process. If the Board determines, at any time, to compensate Directors for service as a Director, the proposed Director Compensation Arrangement shall be reviewed and approved under this Process before any compensation is paid, with particular attention to:

- the implications of Director compensation for the independence of compensated Directors under the Form 990 four-prong test; and
- the disclosure required under the Conflict of Interest Policy.


§9.2. **Officers.** Every officer Compensation Arrangement (including the Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, Grants Lead, or any other officer position) is subject to the full procedure under this Process. Officer Compensation Arrangements shall be reviewed initially before the arrangement takes effect and annually thereafter under §8.2.

§9.3. **Grants Lead.** The Grants Lead is an Officer position under the Grants Lead provisions of the Bylaws, which (under the Officer eligibility provisions of the Bylaws) must be filled by a Director. The Grants Lead's responsibilities are described in the Grants Lead responsibilities provisions of the Grantmaking Policy. If the Board determines, at any time, to compensate the Grants Lead for time devoted to grantmaking duties (whether as a stipend, hourly arrangement, or other form), the Compensation Arrangement shall be reviewed and approved under this Process before any compensation is paid. The Independent Body shall consider whether compensation of the Grants Lead would affect the Grants Lead's status as an Independent Director under §3.4 and shall coordinate any change with the Grantmaking Policy.

§9.4. **Contractors.** Independent contractor arrangements are subject to this Process only where the contractor is a Disqualified Person within the meaning of §3.1. Routine contractor arrangements with persons who are not Disqualified Persons may be approved in accordance with ordinary procurement procedures established by the Board or by management. Where any contractor arrangement involves a Disqualified Person — or where any contractor arrangement, regardless of the contractor's status, exceeds dollar thresholds the Board may from time to time establish for compensation review purposes — the arrangement shall be reviewed and approved under this Process before any payment is made.


## §10. Coordination with Conflict of Interest Policy

§10.1. **Disclosure obligation.** Any Director with a conflict of interest in a Compensation Arrangement under review shall disclose the conflict to the Independent Body in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy. Disclosure shall occur as soon as the Director becomes aware of the conflict, and in any case before any deliberation by the Independent Body on the arrangement.

§10.2. **Recusal.** Any Director with a conflict of interest in the Compensation Arrangement under review:

§10.2.1. shall not be a member of the Independent Body for purposes of the arrangement;

§10.2.2. shall not be present during the deliberation or vote of the Independent Body on the arrangement; and

§10.2.3. shall not seek to influence the deliberation or decision of the Independent Body through any means.

§10.3. **Factual questions.** A Director with a conflict of interest may, at the invitation of the Independent Body, answer factual questions concerning the proposed Compensation Arrangement (including the nature and scope of the services to be rendered) before the deliberation begins, and shall thereafter absent themselves from the meeting in accordance with §10.2.2. The fact that factual questions were posed and answered, the substance of the questions, and the Director's absence from deliberation shall be recorded in the minutes.

§10.4. **Consistency with Conflict of Interest Policy.** This §10 is intended to be consistent with the disclosure and recusal procedures of the Conflict of Interest Policy. In the event of any inconsistency between this Section and the Conflict of Interest Policy with respect to a Compensation Arrangement, the more stringent provision governs.

## §11. Records and Reporting

§11.1. **Records retention.** The Corporation shall retain, with respect to each Compensation Arrangement reviewed under this Process:

§11.1.1. The minutes or other contemporaneous documentation prepared under §7;

§11.1.2. The Comparability Data relied upon under §6;

§11.1.3. Any independent compensation study or consultant report obtained;

§11.1.4. Disclosures of conflicts of interest and documentation of recusal under §10; and

§11.1.5. The terms of the Compensation Arrangement as approved.

These records shall be maintained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

§11.2. **Annual report to the Board.** The Independent Body (or, where the Independent Body is the full Board acting through its Independent Directors, the Secretary) shall report to the full Board no less than annually summarizing all Compensation Arrangements approved or reviewed during the prior year, the Comparability Data relied upon, and the basis for the Independent Body's determination of reasonableness.

§11.3. **Form 990 reporting.** Compensation paid under each Compensation Arrangement shall be reported on the Corporation's Form 990 in accordance with the Form 990 instructions, including (as applicable) Part VII (Compensation of Officers, Directors, Trustees, Key Employees, and Highest Compensated Employees) and Schedule J (Compensation Information). The Treasurer (or other officer responsible for tax filings) shall coordinate with the Corporation's preparer to ensure that the documentation prepared under this Process supports the relevant Form 990 disclosures.

## §12. Violations

§12.1. **First-tier excise tax on the Disqualified Person.** Under Section 4958(a)(1) of the Code, a Disqualified Person who receives an Excess Benefit Transaction is subject to an excise tax equal to twenty-five percent (25%) of the excess benefit. This excise tax is the personal liability of the Disqualified Person and is not paid by the Corporation.

§12.2. **First-tier excise tax on organization managers.** Under Section 4958(a)(2) of the Code, an organization manager (which includes any Director, officer, or other person with authority to act on behalf of the Corporation in approving the transaction) who knowingly participates in an Excess Benefit Transaction is subject to an excise tax equal to ten percent (10%) of the excess benefit, capped at $20,000 per transaction. The excise tax is the personal liability of the organization manager and is not paid by the Corporation.

§12.3. **Second-tier excise tax.** Under Section 4958(b) of the Code, if an Excess Benefit Transaction is not corrected within the taxable period (generally, by the date the Internal Revenue Service mails a notice of deficiency for the first-tier tax), an additional excise tax equal to two hundred percent (200%) of the excess benefit is imposed on the Disqualified Person. Correction generally requires the Disqualified Person to undo the transaction to the extent possible and to make the Corporation whole.

§12.4. **Foundation-level consequences.** Repeated or egregious Excess Benefit Transactions, or compensation practices that the Internal Revenue Service determines reflect a pattern of private inurement or more-than-incidental private benefit, may jeopardize the Corporation's exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code. The Corporation's exemption is the sine qua non of its existence; this Process is one of the principal procedural protections against that risk.

§12.5. **Reporting to the Board.** Any actual, suspected, or alleged Excess Benefit Transaction — and any failure to comply with this Process — shall be reported promptly to the Board (and, where appropriate, to outside counsel) for evaluation, correction, and (if warranted) disclosure on the Corporation's Form 990.

## §13. Amendment

This Process may be amended, in whole or in part, by resolution of the Board, provided that no amendment shall be inconsistent with the Bylaws, the Articles of Incorporation, the Conflict of Interest Policy, or applicable law. Any amendment to the Independent Director definition in §3.4 shall be coordinated with the corresponding definitions in the Director Nomination Policy and the Grantmaking Policy and shall be considered for adoption across all three policies at the same Board meeting.
