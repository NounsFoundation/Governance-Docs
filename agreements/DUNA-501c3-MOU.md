# Memorandum of Understanding

## Between Nouns DAO and Nouns Foundation, Inc.

**Effective Date:** [Date of Execution by Both Parties]

## MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING

**Between:**

**Nouns DAO**, a Wyoming Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association formed under Wyo. Stat. §§ 17-32-101 *et seq.* and operating in accordance with the Nouns DAO Bylaws as adopted by Proposal 727 ("**Nouns DAO**"); acting through its Administrators identified in the Nouns DAO Bylaws (the "**Administrators**"),

**and**

**Nouns Foundation, Inc.**, a Delaware nonprofit corporation recognized as exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (or having an application for such recognition pending) (the "**Corporation**"); acting through the chair of its Board of Directors,

(each, a "**Party**" and together, the "**Parties**").

**Effective Date:** [Date of Execution by Both Parties]

---

### Recitals

A. The Corporation is an independent charitable organization with its own mission, governance, finances, and decision-making authority. The Corporation's mission is to advance the public good through charitable grants and related activities in four areas:

- **Civic engagement and democratic participation** — supporting the study, development, and deployment of decentralized governance technologies and other initiatives that increase informed public participation in governance and strengthen civic institutions;
- **Public goods, science, and technology** — advancing science and education through grants for open-source infrastructure, freely-licensed public technologies, and scientific research, including research conducted through decentralized or open-collaboration models;
- **Arts and culture** — supporting the creation, presentation, and preservation of artistic and cultural works, including grants to individual artists and arts organizations; and
- **Community welfare** — supporting grassroots, community-based organizations and direct-impact initiatives that promote social welfare and serve communities and people in need.

B. Nouns DAO is a separate legal entity formed under Wyoming law with its own membership, governance, treasury, and decision-making authority.

C. The Corporation's origin traces to three Nouns DAO proposals. The Engagement Proposal (Nouns DAO Proposal [No. ___]) funded a feasibility study by Wylie Advisory and 501Foundry into whether an independent §501(c)(3) public charity could be formed to operate alongside Nouns DAO as a separate entity. The Formation Proposal (Nouns DAO Proposal [No. ___]) funded the formation work the study recommended. The MOU Authorization Proposal (Nouns DAO Proposal [No. ___]) authorized the Administrators to enter into this Memorandum on Nouns DAO's behalf.

The Corporation was formed under Delaware law by the act of its Incorporator, who filed the Certificate of Incorporation and seated the initial Board. Nouns DAO's role in the Corporation's formation was that of sponsor and funder: supportive in posture, material in substance, and without authority over whether, when, or how the Corporation came into existence. The Corporation and Nouns DAO are, from inception, separate legal entities. Nouns DAO did not incorporate the Corporation, and Nouns DAO holds no continuing authority over the Corporation's governance or operations.

D. The Parties wish to memorialize a shared understanding of:

- how Nouns DAO may, from time to time, communicate non-binding grant recommendations to the Corporation;
- the architecture by which information flows between the Parties; and
- the principles of independence, separateness, and mutual respect that govern the relationship between the Parties.

E. **The Parties intend that this Memorandum is a non-binding statement of mutual understandings and not a contract, partnership, joint venture, agency, fiduciary relationship, or other legally enforceable arrangement.** The Parties wish to support the Corporation's posture as an independent charitable organization for federal tax, state regulatory, and audit purposes; this Memorandum is intended to clarify that posture, not to qualify it.

---

### §1. Identification and Independence of the Parties

The Parties acknowledge that:

§1.1. The Corporation and Nouns DAO are separate legal entities with their own governance, finances, and decision-making authority;

§1.2. Neither Party has the power to elect, appoint, remove, suspend, or replace any director, officer, member, or governance participant of the other Party;

§1.3. Neither Party has the power to approve, veto, condition, or compel any decision of the other Party;

§1.4. The Corporation conducts its operations, including its grantmaking activities, in the exercise of its Board of Directors' independent fiduciary discretion under applicable law and the Corporation's Bylaws and policies; and

§1.5. Nouns DAO conducts its operations and governance under its own Bylaws, independent of the Corporation.

### §2. Recommendation Flow

§2.1. Nouns DAO may, from time to time, communicate to the Corporation a recommendation that the Corporation consider making a charitable grant to a specified recipient or class of recipients for a specified charitable purpose (a "**Grant Recommendation**").

§2.2. Grant Recommendations are made by Nouns DAO through the passage of an on-chain proposal under Nouns DAO's standard governance process. As of the Effective Date, Nouns DAO conducts governance through the Nouns DAO Governor smart contract deployed at `0x6f3E6272A167e8AcCb32072d08E0957F9c79223d` on Ethereum mainnet, with proxy implementation upgradable under Nouns DAO's existing governance procedures (the "**Nouns DAO Governor**"). In the event Nouns DAO upgrades, replaces, or forks the contract or contracts through which it conducts its governance, the Corporation shall recognize as the operative Nouns DAO Governor those contracts identified as such by the Administrators acting under Wyo. Stat. §§ 17-32-101 *et seq.* and the Nouns DAO Bylaws. The Parties intend that the on-chain proposal carry public-safe content only, as further described in §3.

§2.3. Grant Recommendations are non-binding inputs that the Corporation considers in the exercise of its Board of Directors' independent fiduciary discretion under the Corporation's Bylaws, Grantmaking Policy, and other applicable policies. The Corporation:

§2.3.1. is not obligated to accept any Grant Recommendation;

§2.3.2. may decline any Grant Recommendation, in whole or in part, without providing reasons beyond what the Corporation's Grantmaking Policy requires;

§2.3.3. may modify the terms of any Grant Recommendation, including by adjusting the amount, timing, conditions, or recipient of any grant, in the Board's discretion;

§2.3.4. may make grants to recipients not the subject of any Grant Recommendation; and

§2.3.5. may decline to consider any Grant Recommendation that, in the Corporation's sole judgment, would not further the Corporation's exempt purposes, would create undue compliance or examination risk, or would otherwise be inconsistent with the Corporation's policies or charitable mission.

§2.4. The Corporation's consideration of Grant Recommendations does not establish the Corporation as a donor-advised fund within the meaning of IRC § 4966 with respect to Nouns DAO, the Administrators, or any individual Nouns DAO participant. The Corporation reserves the right to decline to consider any communication that, in the Corporation's sole judgment, would create such a characterization.

### §3. Two-Layer Submission Architecture

The Parties intend that information relating to Grant Recommendations flow through two layers, in order to keep personally identifiable information off-chain:

§3.1. **Public on-chain layer.** The Nouns DAO on-chain proposal communicating a Grant Recommendation shall carry only public-safe content, including:

- a description of the proposed charitable activity;
- the type of recipient (e.g., 501(c)(3) public charity, governmental unit, individual under §4945(g), etc.);
- the proposed amount;
- the proposed use of funds;
- the charitable class served; and
- any disclosed conflicts of interest of the proposer or supporters.

§3.2. **Private post-passage intake layer.** Personally identifiable information regarding the proposed recipient flows directly to the Corporation through a private intake process operated by the Corporation. Such information includes:

- legal name;
- address;
- Employer Identification Number (if applicable);
- KYC documentation;
- banking information for disbursement; and
- any other information required by the Corporation's Grantmaking Policy or Gift Acceptance Policy.

The Corporation may rely on KYC and identity-verification infrastructure operated by the Administrators, including Persona or any successor service, to support its diligence.

### §4. No Money Flow

§4.1. Nouns DAO is not a routine funder of the Corporation. The Corporation's funding model is self-fundraising; the Corporation does not anticipate receiving recurring financial support from Nouns DAO.

§4.2. Any future financial support from Nouns DAO to the Corporation, or from the Corporation to Nouns DAO, shall be evaluated by the recipient Party under its applicable policies (including, in the case of the Corporation, its Gift Acceptance Policy, Grantmaking Policy, and the public support test implications under IRC § 509(a)) and is not authorized, anticipated, or required by this Memorandum.

§4.3. The Corporation receives no financial consideration, fee, payment, transfer of value, or other economic benefit from Nouns DAO under or in connection with this Memorandum.

### §5. Brand and Intellectual Property

§5.1. The Parties acknowledge that the Nouns brand, the Nouns artwork, and all derivative iconography ("**Nouns IP**") are released by Nouns DAO into the public domain under the Creative Commons CC0 1.0 Universal Public Domain Dedication and are not subject to any license, royalty, or restriction.

§5.2. The Corporation's use of any Nouns IP in connection with its charitable activities is independent of this Memorandum and is not authorized by, conditioned on, or made on behalf of Nouns DAO. Nothing in this Memorandum grants any license, permission, restriction, or other right of any nature with respect to Nouns IP.

### §6. Conflicts and Overlap

§6.1. The Parties acknowledge that one or more directors, officers, or other governance participants of the Corporation may also hold Nouns or otherwise participate in Nouns DAO governance, and that one or more Administrators or other governance participants of Nouns DAO may also serve the Corporation in some capacity.

§6.2. The Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy governs the disclosure, evaluation, and management of any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest involving the Corporation. Each director, officer, and key employee of the Corporation is responsible for compliance with that Policy.

§6.3. Nothing in this Memorandum modifies, qualifies, or supplements the duties of any director, officer, or key employee of the Corporation under the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy or under applicable law.

### §7. Communication

§7.1. Routine communication between the Parties under this Memorandum may occur through the Nouns DAO governance forum, propdates, the Corporation's website, email, or other reasonable means.

§7.2. Formal communication regarding the operation, amendment, or termination of this Memorandum shall be in writing and addressed to:

- the chair of the Corporation's Board of Directors at the Corporation's principal mailing address; and
- the Administrators at the contact information then designated in the Nouns DAO Bylaws or governance materials.

### §8. Term and Termination

§8.1. This Memorandum is effective on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either Party.

§8.2. Either Party may terminate this Memorandum, with or without cause, by providing not less than thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party.

§8.3. Termination of this Memorandum does not affect the validity of any grant previously made by the Corporation, the Corporation's continuing obligations under any existing grant agreement, or the independence of either Party from the other.

### §9. Non-Binding Character

§9.1. **The Parties intend that this Memorandum is a non-binding statement of mutual understandings and is not intended to create, and shall not create, any contract, partnership, joint venture, agency, employment, fiduciary, trust, or other legally enforceable relationship between the Parties.**

§9.2. No provision of this Memorandum is intended to be enforceable in law or equity, and no Party is entitled to specific performance, damages, injunctive relief, restitution, or other remedy under or in connection with this Memorandum.

§9.3. No consideration, monetary or otherwise, has been exchanged between the Parties in connection with this Memorandum.

§9.4. The Parties expressly disclaim any intention that this Memorandum be construed as a contract, and no course of dealing, course of performance, or usage between the Parties shall be construed to create any binding obligation under this Memorandum.

§9.5. The Parties may, by separate writing, enter into legally binding agreements with respect to specific matters; any such agreement shall be evidenced by an instrument that explicitly states the Parties' intention to be legally bound.

### §10. Miscellaneous

§10.1. **No third-party beneficiaries.** This Memorandum is solely for the benefit of the Parties and does not create any rights in any third party.

§10.2. **Amendment.** This Memorandum may be amended only by a writing signed by the chair of the Corporation's Board of Directors and the Administrators on behalf of Nouns DAO.

§10.3. **Severability.** If any provision of this Memorandum is determined to be unenforceable for any reason, the remaining provisions shall continue in effect to the extent consistent with the Parties' intent.

§10.4. **Governing principles.** The Parties intend that this Memorandum be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, to the extent that any construction is required, but reaffirm that the Memorandum is not intended to be legally enforceable.

§10.5. **Entire understanding.** This Memorandum sets forth the entire mutual understanding of the Parties with respect to the subject matter of this Memorandum and supersedes any prior or contemporaneous communication or understanding between the Parties on the same subject matter.

§10.6. **Counterparts; electronic signature.** This Memorandum may be executed in counterparts, each of which is an original and all of which together constitute one instrument. Signatures delivered electronically have the same effect as original signatures.
