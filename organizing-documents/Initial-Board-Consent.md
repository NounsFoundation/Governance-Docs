# Action by Unanimous Written Consent of the Initial Board of Directors

## Nouns Foundation, Inc.

A Delaware non-stock corporation

---

**Effective Date:** [DATE TO BE INSERTED ON EXECUTION]

The undersigned, constituting all of the initial Directors of Nouns Foundation, Inc., a Delaware non-stock corporation (the "**Corporation**"), acting by unanimous written consent in lieu of an organizational meeting of the Board of Directors of the Corporation (the "**Board**") pursuant to Section 141(f) of the Delaware General Corporation Law, 8 Del. C. § 141(f) (the "**DGCL**"), and pursuant to Article III §16 of the Bylaws of the Corporation, hereby adopt the following resolutions:

---

## Recitals

**A.** The Certificate of Incorporation of the Corporation (the "**Certificate**") was filed with the Office of the Secretary of State of the State of Delaware on [DATE OF FILING], evidenced by Delaware Secretary of State file number [FILE NUMBER], and the Corporation came into existence on such date in accordance with DGCL § 106.

**B.** By Action by Written Consent of the Sole Incorporator dated [DATE OF INCORPORATOR CONSENT] (the "**Incorporator Consent**"), the sole incorporator of the Corporation (i) acknowledged the filing of the Certificate, (ii) fixed the initial number of Directors at five (5), (iii) adopted the initial Bylaws of the Corporation (the "**Bylaws**"), (iv) appointed the undersigned as the initial Directors, (v) allocated the initial Directors to staggered initial terms of one (1), two (2), or three (3) years in accordance with Article III §3.2 of the Bylaws, and (vi) resigned as incorporator.

**C.** Each of the undersigned has accepted office as an initial Director, has delivered the certification regarding the Office of Foreign Assets Control Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List required by Article III §2.2 of the Bylaws, and is qualified to serve as a Director under the Bylaws and applicable law.

**D.** The Board now desires to complete the organization of the Corporation by ratifying the foregoing, electing officers, adopting governance policies, designating a fiscal year and principal office of operations, authorizing the application for federal income tax exemption under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "**Code**"), and authorizing such other actions as are necessary or appropriate to commence the operations of the Corporation.

---

## 1. Acceptance of Office; Ratification of Incorporator Action

**RESOLVED**, that each of the undersigned hereby acknowledges and accepts service as an initial Director of the Corporation, in accordance with the appointment made under the Incorporator Consent; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that all actions taken by the sole incorporator under the Incorporator Consent — including the filing of the Certificate, the fixing of the initial number of Directors, the adoption of the Bylaws, the appointment of the initial Directors, and the allocation of staggered initial terms — are hereby in all respects ratified, confirmed, and approved as actions of the Corporation; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Certificate and the Bylaws, in the forms filed and adopted respectively under the Incorporator Consent, are hereby in all respects ratified and confirmed as the governing instruments of the Corporation, and that the Secretary, upon election pursuant to §3 of this Consent, is directed to maintain originals of the Certificate and the Bylaws in the minute book of the Corporation as part of the permanent corporate records.

## 2. Designation of Principal Office of Operations

**RESOLVED**, that, in addition to the registered office and registered agent in the State of Delaware established under the Certificate, the principal office of operations of the Corporation, for purposes of conducting the business of the Corporation and receiving correspondence (other than legal process directed to the registered agent), shall initially be at:

> [PRINCIPAL OFFICE OF OPERATIONS — STREET ADDRESS, CITY, STATE, ZIP]

and the Board may, by resolution, from time to time change the location of the principal office of operations or establish additional offices, all consistent with Article I §§ 2 and 3 of the Bylaws.

## 4. Designation of Fiscal Year

**RESOLVED**, that, consistent with Article IX §1 of the Bylaws, the fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year, beginning on January 1 and ending on December 31 of each year; provided, however, that the first fiscal year of the Corporation shall begin on the date of filing of the Certificate and end on December 31, [YEAR OF INCORPORATION].

## 5. Initial Conflict of Interest Disclosures

**RESOLVED**, that each Director and each officer of the Corporation (whether elected under §3 of this Consent or hereafter) shall submit to the Secretary an initial written Conflict of Interest disclosure under the Conflict of Interest Policy adopted in §6 of this Consent within thirty (30) days after the later of (i) the Effective Date of this Consent and (ii) the date on which such Director or officer takes office; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Secretary is directed to (i) maintain the initial and subsequent annual Conflict of Interest disclosures in the corporate records, (ii) report any material disclosures to the Board for action under the Conflict of Interest Policy, and (iii) confirm to the Board on or before each annual meeting that all required disclosures have been collected.

## 7. Acknowledgment of DUNA / 501(c)(3) Memorandum of Understanding Framework

**WHEREAS**, the Board has reviewed the form of Memorandum of Understanding Between Nouns DAO (the "**DUNA**") and the Corporation (the "**DUNA-501c3 MOU**") attached to this Consent as **Exhibit H**, which memorializes the non-binding character of charitable recommendations originating from the DUNA, the Corporation's exclusive and independent discretion over all grant decisions, the absence of appointment, removal, or veto authority on the part of the DUNA over the Corporation, and related structural matters; and

**WHEREAS**, the parties' shared working understanding is that the DUNA Administrators are expected to seek explicit Nouns DAO proposal authorization prior to executing the DUNA-501c3 MOU on behalf of the DUNA, and that the DUNA-501c3 MOU shall become effective on the later of (i) its execution by both parties and (ii) any later effective date stated in the executed Memorandum;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Board acknowledges the form of the DUNA-501c3 MOU as the structural framework intended to govern the operational relationship between the Corporation and the DUNA upon execution; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Chair is hereby authorized, but not directed, to execute the DUNA-501c3 MOU on behalf of the Corporation, in such form as the Chair, after consultation with legal counsel and following further review by the Board, shall determine to be in the best interests of the Corporation, contingent upon prior authorization of execution by the DUNA in accordance with the DUNA's governance procedures.

## 8. Authorization to Apply for Employer Identification Number

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall apply to the Internal Revenue Service for an Employer Identification Number ("**EIN**") on Form SS-4 (Application for Employer Identification Number), or by such other application method as the Internal Revenue Service may then prescribe; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Treasurer is hereby authorized and directed (and the Chair is hereby authorized, in the Treasurer's absence) to execute and file the Form SS-4 application, to act as the responsible party listed on such application, and to take such further actions as may be necessary or appropriate to obtain the EIN, with the resulting EIN to be reported to the Board and entered in the corporate records upon issuance.

## 9. Authorization to Establish Bank Accounts

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall establish one or more operating bank accounts at one or more federally insured financial institutions selected by the Treasurer, in consultation with the Chair, to facilitate the receipt, custody, and disbursement of the Corporation's funds, all in accordance with Article IX §§ 2, 3, and 5 of the Bylaws; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Treasurer (and, in the Treasurer's absence, the Chair) is hereby authorized, acting alone, to (i) open such accounts in the name of the Corporation, (ii) execute on behalf of the Corporation any banking resolutions, signature cards, account agreements, electronic banking authorizations, and similar documents required by the financial institution, including standard form corporate resolutions adopted by such institution, and (iii) designate such officers and other authorized signers as the Treasurer shall determine, subject to the following:

  (a) no single signer shall have unilateral authority to disburse Corporation funds in excess of [INITIAL DISBURSEMENT THRESHOLD — RECOMMEND $5,000] without the countersignature of a second authorized signer or the prior approval of the Board; and

  (b) the Treasurer shall report the selected financial institution, the form of resolutions executed, and the designated signers and authorization thresholds to the Board at the Board's next meeting following account opening, for ratification at that meeting; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Secretary of the financial institution(s) at which the Corporation maintains accounts is requested to accept a certified copy of this §9 (or any standard form banking resolutions executed pursuant hereto) as the authorization of the Corporation to act as set forth herein, and that such authorization shall continue in force until written notice of revocation is received by the financial institution from the Corporation.

## 10. Authorization to Establish On-Chain Treasury (Multisig)

**RESOLVED**, that, in addition to the bank accounts authorized in §9 of this Consent, the Corporation shall establish on-chain treasury arrangements at one or more digital-asset custody platforms or smart-contract-based custody arrangements, structured as a multi-signature arrangement requiring the affirmative authorization of not fewer than three (3) of five (5) designated signers, all in accordance with Article IX §2.2 of the Bylaws; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Treasurer (and, in the Treasurer's absence, the Chair) is hereby authorized to (i) cause the Corporation to enter into such custody, smart-contract, and platform agreements as are necessary to establish the on-chain treasury, (ii) cause the Corporation to leverage existing know-your-customer ("**KYC**") infrastructure operated for the benefit of the DUNA (including the Persona integration) to the extent operationally efficient and consistent with the Corporation's compliance posture, and (iii) report the selected platform(s), the configuration of the multi-signature arrangement, and the proposed slate of signers to the Board at the Board's next meeting, with formal designation of the signers to be made by Board resolution at that meeting.

## 11. Authorization to Apply for Recognition of Exemption (Form 1023)

**WHEREAS**, the Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and other purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, as set forth in the Certificate and in Article I §4 of the Bylaws; and

**WHEREAS**, the Board has determined that it is in the best interests of the Corporation to apply to the Internal Revenue Service for recognition of exemption from federal income taxation under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code on the basis that the Corporation is, and shall be operated as, a publicly supported organization classified as a public charity under Section 509(a)(1) or Section 509(a)(2) of the Code (with the precise classification to be selected at the time of filing in consultation with legal counsel based on the Corporation's anticipated funding profile);

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall prepare and file with the Internal Revenue Service Form 1023, Application for Recognition of Exemption Under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code (the "**Form 1023**"), seeking such recognition; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Corporation hereby engages legal counsel — Cahill Gordon & Reindel LLP and the 501Foundry pro bono initiative thereof ("**501Foundry**") — to prepare, finalize, and file the Form 1023 on behalf of the Corporation, in accordance with the Engagement Letter previously executed between the Corporation (or its predecessor sponsoring entity) and Cahill Gordon & Reindel LLP; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Chair is hereby authorized and directed (i) to execute the Form 1023, including any schedules, attachments, and supplementary narratives, on behalf of the Corporation, as the principal officer of the Corporation for purposes of signing documents requiring an executive signature on behalf of the Corporation under Article IV §4.1 of the Bylaws, (ii) to provide such factual representations, certifications, and supporting documentation to legal counsel as may be necessary or appropriate to the preparation of the Form 1023, and (iii) to take such further actions as may be necessary or appropriate to obtain a determination letter recognizing the Corporation's exemption under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Treasurer is hereby authorized to disburse from the Corporation's funds the user fee required by the Internal Revenue Service in connection with the Form 1023 filing (currently $600), together with any incidental disbursements related to the application; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Chair, the Treasurer, and the Secretary are each authorized to respond, with the assistance of legal counsel, to any inquiries, requests for additional information, or examination correspondence from the Internal Revenue Service in connection with the Form 1023, and to take such further actions as may be necessary or appropriate to obtain and maintain the Corporation's recognition of exemption under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

## 12. Authorization for State Charitable Solicitation Registration and Other State Compliance Filings

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation shall register, file, or qualify with the appropriate governmental authority in each state in which (i) the Corporation conducts charitable solicitation activity of a nature or scale that requires registration, (ii) the Corporation holds assets requiring registration with the state attorney general or other charitable supervisor, or (iii) the Corporation is otherwise required by applicable law to register, file, or qualify (collectively, "**State Charitable Compliance Filings**"); and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Chair, the Treasurer, and the Secretary are each hereby authorized to identify, prepare, and file (or to engage a registered agent or compliance vendor to prepare and file) such State Charitable Compliance Filings, including, in the first instance, registration with the California Attorney General's Registry of Charities and Fundraisers and such other state filings as the Chair, in consultation with the Secretary and legal counsel, shall determine to be required by applicable law or appropriate in light of the Corporation's anticipated solicitation footprint; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Secretary shall report to the Board, at or before the Board's next regular meeting, on the State Charitable Compliance Filings made, in process, or anticipated, together with the associated compliance calendar and budget.

## 13. Authorization to Procure Directors' and Officers' Liability Insurance

**RESOLVED**, that, consistent with Article VI §5 of the Bylaws, the Corporation shall procure and maintain a policy of directors' and officers' liability insurance (a "**D&O Policy**") on behalf of the Directors and officers of the Corporation; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Chair and the Treasurer are each hereby authorized to (i) solicit quotations from one or more reputable insurance carriers, (ii) bind a D&O Policy on behalf of the Corporation on such terms as the Chair and the Treasurer, after consultation with legal counsel, shall jointly determine to be reasonable and consistent with the operational risk profile of the Corporation, and (iii) report the selected carrier, policy limits, premium, and material policy terms to the Board at the Board's next meeting for ratification.

## 14. Engagement of Professional Service Providers

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation hereby ratifies, confirms, and approves the engagement of Cahill Gordon & Reindel LLP (through 501Foundry) as legal counsel to the Corporation for formation and Section 501(c)(3) exemption matters, in accordance with the Engagement Letter previously delivered by Cahill Gordon & Reindel LLP; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Chair is hereby authorized to engage, on terms the Chair determines to be reasonable, such additional professional service providers as may be necessary or appropriate for the Corporation's operations, including (i) an accountant or bookkeeper, (ii) state-registered agents in jurisdictions where required, (iii) compliance vendors for State Charitable Compliance Filings, and (iv) such other professionals as the Board may from time to time direct, with each such engagement to be reported to the Board at the Board's next meeting following engagement.

## 15. Reimbursement of Pre-Incorporation and Organizational Expenses

**RESOLVED**, that the Corporation hereby authorizes the reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred on behalf of the Corporation, or for the benefit of the Corporation, prior to the Effective Date of this Consent (including, by way of example and without limitation, the Delaware Secretary of State filing fee paid in connection with the filing of the Certificate, the Internal Revenue Service user fee anticipated to be paid in connection with the Form 1023, and any other out-of-pocket disbursements made for the organizational benefit of the Corporation), upon (i) submission of reasonable documentation of such expenses to the Treasurer and (ii) verification by the Treasurer that such expenses were incurred for the benefit of the Corporation; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that any such reimbursement shall be reflected in the Corporation's books and records as reimbursement of organizational expenses, and not as compensation to the person reimbursed, and that no such reimbursement shall be made to any Director, officer, or related person except in accordance with the COI Policy and, where applicable, the Compensation Review Process.

## 16. Acknowledgment Regarding Wylie Advisory Engagement and Service of wylin.eth

## 17. Direction Regarding Independent Director Majority and Board Recruitment

**RESOLVED**, that the Board reaffirms the Bylaws requirement (Article III §2.3) that a majority of the Directors then in office shall at all times be Independent Directors (as defined in Article III §2.4 of the Bylaws, which definition tracks the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the Internal Revenue Service instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b); and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the Board confirms that the composition of the initial Board, with three (3) Independent Directors out of five (5), satisfies the Independent Director majority requirement, and that any subsequent change in the composition of the Board — whether by resignation, removal, expiration of term, expansion of the Board, or change in the Independent Director status of any Director — shall be evaluated under, and shall conform to, Article III §§ 2(c) and 2(d) of the Bylaws, including the ninety- (90-) day cure period under Article III §2.3 for filling a vacancy arising from the loss of an Independent Director.

## 18. Omnibus Authorization

**RESOLVED**, that the Chair, the Treasurer, the Secretary, and the Grants Lead — each acting alone within the scope of such officer's duties under the Bylaws — are hereby authorized and directed to take such further actions, to execute and deliver such further instruments and documents, to make such further filings, and to pay such further fees and expenses (in each case, on behalf of and in the name of the Corporation) as such officer shall determine to be necessary, appropriate, or convenient to carry out the intent and accomplish the purposes of the foregoing resolutions; and

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that all actions previously taken by any Director, by any prospective officer, or by any agent of the Corporation in furtherance of the foregoing resolutions are hereby in all respects ratified, confirmed, and approved as actions of the Corporation.
