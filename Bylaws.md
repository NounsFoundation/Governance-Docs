# Bylaws

## Nouns Foundation

**Effective:** [Date of Board Adoption]

## Article I — Name, Offices, and Purpose

### §1. Name

The name of the corporation is **Nouns Foundation, Inc.** (the "**Corporation**"). The Corporation is a nonprofit nonstock corporation organized under the General Corporation Law of the State of Delaware (the "**DGCL**"), Title 8 of the Delaware Code.

### §2. Principal Office

§2.1. The registered office of the Corporation in the State of Delaware shall be located at the address set forth in the Corporation's Certificate of Incorporation (as it may be amended or restated from time to time, the "**Certificate**"), and the registered agent at such address shall be the person designated in the Certificate.

§2.2. The principal place of business of the Corporation may be located within or outside the State of Delaware, as the Board of Directors (the "**Board**") may from time to time designate by resolution. The Corporation may operate primarily through remote and distributed means and is not required to maintain a fixed physical principal place of business.

### §3. Other Offices

The Corporation may have such other offices, within or outside the State of Delaware, as the Board may from time to time designate or as the affairs of the Corporation may require.

### §4. Charitable Purpose

§4.1. The Corporation is organized and shall be operated exclusively for charitable, educational, and other purposes described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "**Code**"), and the regulations promulgated thereunder.

§4.2. The Corporation's mission is to advance the public good through charitable grants and related activities in four areas:

- **Civic engagement and democratic participation** — supporting the study, development, and deployment of decentralized governance technologies and other initiatives that increase informed public participation in governance and strengthen civic institutions;
- **Public goods, science, and technology** — advancing science and education through grants for open-source infrastructure, freely-licensed public technologies, and scientific research, including research conducted through decentralized or open-collaboration models;
- **Arts and culture** — supporting the creation, presentation, and preservation of artistic and cultural works, including grants to individual artists and arts organizations; and
- **Community welfare** — supporting grassroots, community-based organizations and direct-impact initiatives that promote social welfare and serve communities and people in need.

The Corporation may pursue this mission by making grants to other organizations and to individuals, by supporting charitable activities through fiscal sponsorship and similar arrangements (when and to the extent the Board authorizes such activities), and by engaging in such other charitable, educational, and related activities as the Board may from time to time approve consistent with the Corporation's tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3).

§4.3. The purposes set forth in this §4 are intended to be construed broadly to permit the Corporation to engage in any lawful activity for which a nonprofit corporation may be organized under the DGCL and that is consistent with Section 501(c)(3).

### §5. Limitations on Activities

§5.1. **Private inurement.** No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, any Director, officer, or other private person, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in §4.

§5.2. **Lobbying.** No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, except as permitted under Section 501(c)(3) and any applicable election under Section 501(h) of the Code.

§5.3. **Political campaign intervention.** The Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distributing of statements), any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

§5.4. **Permitted activities.** Notwithstanding any other provision of these Bylaws, the Corporation shall not carry on any activity not permitted to be carried on:

- by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code; or
- by a corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code.

## Article II — Members

### §1. No Members

The Corporation shall have no members within the meaning of Section 102(a)(4) or Section 215 of the DGCL or any successor provision. All rights, powers, and authority that would otherwise be vested in members of a nonstock corporation under the DGCL or otherwise are vested in the Board, and any provision of the DGCL or other applicable law that requires or permits action by members shall, with respect to the Corporation, be satisfied by action of the Board taken in accordance with these Bylaws and the Certificate. References in these Bylaws or in the Certificate to any DGCL provision predicated on the existence of members shall be construed accordingly.

## Article III — Board of Directors

### §1. Powers

The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board, in accordance with Section 141(a) of the DGCL. The Board may exercise all such powers of the Corporation and do all such lawful acts and things as are not by the DGCL, the Certificate, or these Bylaws directed or required to be exercised or done by some other person or body.

### §2. Number, Qualifications, and Composition

§2.1. **Number.** The number of Directors constituting the Board shall be five (5) as of the effective date of these Bylaws and may be increased or decreased from time to time by resolution of the Board, provided that the number of Directors shall be not fewer than three (3) nor more than nine (9).

§2.2. **Qualifications.** Each Director shall be a natural person of full legal age. Directors are not required to be residents of the State of Delaware or to satisfy any other geographic, citizenship, or affiliation requirement, except as the Board may from time to time prescribe by resolution consistent with the DGCL and these Bylaws. No person shall be eligible to serve as a Director if such person appears on the Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List maintained by the Office of Foreign Assets Control of the U.S. Department of the Treasury ("**OFAC**"), or on any other list of persons with whom the Corporation is prohibited from engaging in transactions under U.S. economic sanctions laws administered by OFAC or any successor agency. Each Director shall promptly notify the Board if the Director becomes aware of any fact that would, or reasonably might, affect the Director's qualification under the preceding sentence; upon such notification, the Director shall be deemed to have automatically resigned, and the resulting vacancy shall be filled in accordance with §8 of this Article III.

§2.3. **Independent Director majority.** A majority of the Directors then in office shall at all times be Independent Directors. If the loss of an Independent Director would cause the Board to fall below the Independent Director majority required by this §2.3, the Board shall fill the resulting vacancy with an Independent Director within a reasonable period not to exceed ninety (90) days.

§2.4. **Independent Director defined.** For purposes of these Bylaws, "**Independent Director**" means a Director who satisfies all four of the following conditions throughout the relevant period:

§2.4.1. The Director did not receive compensation as an officer or other employee of the Corporation or any related organization. Reasonable compensation paid to the Director for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board does not affect this analysis.

§2.4.2. The Director did not receive total compensation or other payments from the Corporation and any related organizations as an independent contractor exceeding $10,000 during the year. Reimbursement of reasonable expenses under an accountable plan, and reasonable compensation for services performed in the capacity as a member of the Board, are not counted toward the $10,000 threshold.

§2.4.3. Neither the Director nor any Family Member of the Director was involved in a transaction with the Corporation, whether directly or indirectly through affiliation with another organization, of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Corporation's Form 990 (Transactions with Interested Persons).

§2.4.4. Neither the Director nor any Family Member of the Director was involved in a transaction, whether directly or indirectly, with a taxable or tax-exempt related organization of a type and amount required to be reported on Schedule L of the Form 990 of that related organization.

For §2.4.3 and §2.4.4, Schedule L transactions include excess benefit transactions, loans to or from interested persons, grants or assistance benefiting interested persons, and business transactions involving interested persons. For purposes of this §2.4, "**Family Member**" means, with respect to any Director, the Director's spouse or domestic partner; the Director's parents, children, siblings, grandparents, and grandchildren; the spouses, domestic partners, parents, children, siblings, and grandchildren of any of the foregoing; and any other person residing in the Director's household. The fact that a Director is a donor to the Corporation, in any amount, does not by itself affect the Director's status as an Independent Director. This definition is intended to track the meaning of "independent voting member of the governing body" in the Internal Revenue Service instructions to Form 990, Part VI, Line 1b, as those instructions may be amended from time to time. In the event of any inconsistency between this definition and the Form 990 instructions then in effect, the Form 990 instructions govern for purposes of the Corporation's Form 990 reporting.


### §3. Term

§3.1. **Standard term.** Each Director shall serve a term of three (3) years and until such Director's successor is elected and qualified, or until such Director's earlier death, resignation, or removal.

§3.2. **Founding directors — staggered initial terms.** To produce a Board on which Director term expirations are distributed across multiple years rather than occurring simultaneously, the initial Directors shall serve initial terms of one (1), two (2), or three (3) years as designated in the Corporation's organizational consent. The Board, in adopting that consent, shall allocate the initial terms so that no more than approximately one-third of the initial Director seats expire in any single year. Each subsequent Director (whether elected to fill an expired seat or to a newly created seat upon expansion of the Board) shall serve a standard three-year term under §3.1.


§3.3. **Term limits.** No term limits apply to Director service unless and until the Board adopts term-limit provisions by resolution or by amendment to these Bylaws.

### §4. Election of Directors

§4.1. **Annual election.** Directors whose terms are expiring shall be elected at the annual meeting of the Board held under §10, by the affirmative vote of a majority of the Directors then in office.

§4.2. **Self-perpetuating Board.** The Board is self-perpetuating. Vacancies on the Board, whether occurring by resignation, removal, expiration of term, or expansion of the Board, shall be filled in accordance with §8 and, where applicable, §5.

### §5. Director Nomination Process

§5.1. **Self-Perpetuating Board.**

The Board is self-perpetuating. Vacancies on the Board, whether occurring by resignation, removal, expiration of term, or expansion of the Board, shall be filled by the affirmative vote of a majority of the Directors then in office, in each case in the exercise of the Board's independent fiduciary discretion under the laws of the State of Delaware and these Bylaws.

§5.2. **Director Nomination Process.**

The Board shall establish and maintain a written Director Nomination Policy (the "**Nomination Policy**") that describes the procedures by which the Board solicits, receives, vets, and considers candidates for election or appointment as Directors. The Nomination Policy shall be adopted, amended, and repealed by resolution of the Board and shall not be inconsistent with the provisions of this Section.

§5.3. **Recognition of Nouns DAO Nominations.**

The Nomination Policy shall provide a defined procedure (the "**Director Nomination Process**") under which the Nouns DAO community may submit candidate nominations to the Board for the Board's consideration. Submissions under the Director Nomination Process shall be made through the passage of a Director Nomination Proposal under the standard governance process of the Nouns DAO. Within thirty (30) days following the passage of any such Director Nomination Proposal, the Board (acting through the Board Chair, a Vetting Coordinator designated under the Nomination Policy, or such other Director as the Board may designate) shall:

1. acknowledge receipt of the nomination,
2. conduct the vetting and evaluation procedures set forth in the Nomination Policy, and
3. record in the minutes of the next regular or special meeting of the Board that the nomination was considered, together with the disposition of the nomination.

The Director Nomination Process is one of several methods by which the Board may identify and evaluate candidates for Director service. The Board may identify and evaluate candidates from any other source consistent with the Nomination Policy.

§5.4. **Non-Binding Character; Reservation of Board Authority.**

For the avoidance of doubt:

§5.4.1. **Non-binding nature.** Director nominations submitted under the Director Nomination Process are non-binding inputs that the Board considers in the exercise of its independent fiduciary discretion. The Board is not obligated to elect or appoint any candidate nominated under the Director Nomination Process.

§5.4.2. **No appointment or removal authority.** Nothing in this Section, the Nomination Policy, or any related instrument creates any power, authority, expectation, or right of any individual, group, decentralized governance organization, token holder, or other person external to the Board to elect, appoint, remove, suspend, replace, or otherwise compel the seating or unseating of any Director of the Corporation.

§5.4.3. **No quorum or voting effect.** Submissions under the Director Nomination Process do not create any quorum requirement, voting threshold, or procedural prerequisite for Board action on Director elections or appointments. The Board may elect or appoint Directors, fill vacancies, expand or contract the Board, or take any other action with respect to its composition without regard to the existence, status, or content of any Director Nomination Proposal.

§5.4.4. **Reservation of authority.** The Board retains full and final authority over the selection of Directors of the Corporation, including the affirmative power to decline to elect or appoint any candidate the Board determines to be inappropriate for Director service, regardless of the source of the candidate's recommendation. The provisions of this Section establish a procedural duty of consideration only and do not transfer, share, condition, or limit that authority in any respect.

§5.5. **Cap on Seats Subject to the Director Nomination Process.**

The obligation of consideration set forth in §5.3 shall apply in any calendar year to candidate submissions through the Director Nomination Process for no more than [forty percent (40%)] of the seats then constituting the Board, rounded down. Submissions exceeding this cap do not trigger the obligation of consideration. The Board's independent authority under §5.4 to elect or appoint Directors from any source — including from candidates identified through the Director Nomination Process — is not limited by this cap.

§5.6. **Amendment.**

This Section, including each of §5.1 through §5.5, may be amended only by the affirmative vote of [two-thirds (2/3)] of the Directors then in office at a meeting of the Board for which not less than [thirty (30) days'] prior written notice of the proposed amendment has been given to each Director. The Nomination Policy may be amended by resolution of the Board in the ordinary course.

### §6. Resignation

Any Director may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the Board Chair or the Secretary. The resignation shall be effective upon receipt unless the notice specifies a later effective date or an effective date conditioned upon a future event (in which case the resignation shall be effective in accordance with its terms). Acceptance of a resignation by the Board is not required to make the resignation effective.

### §7. Removal

§7.1. **Standard.** Any Director may be removed, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the Directors then in office at a regular or special meeting of the Board called for the purpose, provided that written notice of the proposed removal has been delivered to each Director (including the Director proposed to be removed) not less than ten (10) days prior to the meeting.

§7.2. **Recusal of Director subject to removal.** The Director who is the subject of the removal action shall not vote on the removal and shall not be counted in determining the two-thirds threshold under §7.1. The Director may, however, attend the meeting and present a response to the proposed removal before the vote is taken.

§7.3. **Effective date.** Removal shall be effective immediately upon the vote, unless the resolution of removal specifies a later effective date.

### §8. Vacancies

§8.1. **General rule.** Except as provided in §8.2, any vacancy on the Board, whether occurring by resignation, removal, death, expiration of term, expansion of the Board, or otherwise, may be filled by the affirmative vote of a majority of the Directors then in office (even if less than a quorum), or by a sole remaining Director.

§8.2. **Vacancy created by removal of a Director seated through the Director Nomination Process.** If the Director whose seat is vacant was elected or appointed pursuant to a Recommendation Proposal considered under §5, the vacancy shall be filled following the procedures of §5 to the extent the Board determines that doing so is operationally feasible within the Board's needs for timely Board composition. Nothing in this §8.2 limits the Board's ultimate authority under §5.4.

§8.3. **Term of person filling vacancy.** A Director elected to fill a vacancy shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired term of the Director whose vacancy is being filled and until such Director's successor is elected and qualified, or until such Director's earlier death, resignation, or removal.

### §9. Compensation

§9.1. **No initial compensation.** As of the effective date of these Bylaws, Directors shall not receive compensation for service in the capacity of Director.

§9.2. **Reimbursement of expenses.** Directors may be reimbursed for ordinary and necessary expenses incurred in connection with the performance of their duties as Directors, in accordance with an accountable expense-reimbursement plan adopted by the Board.

§9.3. **Future Director compensation.** The Board may, by resolution, authorize compensation for service as a Director, provided that any such compensation shall be approved through a procedure designed to qualify for the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness under Section 4958 of the Internal Revenue Code and Treasury Regulation § 53.4958-6. Section 4958 imposes excise taxes on compensation paid by the Corporation to a "Disqualified Person" (any Director, officer, or other person in a position to exercise substantial influence over the Corporation's affairs) to the extent the compensation exceeds reasonable value, and on any organization manager who knowingly approves such an arrangement. The rebuttable presumption procedure provides a safe harbor that, when followed, causes the IRS to presume the compensation is reasonable, shifting the burden of proving unreasonableness to the IRS. The procedure requires:

- approval by an independent body free of conflicts;
- reliance on appropriate comparability data; and
- contemporaneous documentation of the basis for the determination.

The operational details of the procedure are set forth in the Compensation Arrangements provisions of the COI Policy and the Compensation Review Process.


### §10. Annual Meeting

The annual meeting of the Board shall be held each year at such time and place (which may be a physical location, a virtual location, or a combination thereof) as the Board may from time to time determine, for the purposes of electing Directors whose terms are expiring, electing officers, and transacting such other business as may properly come before the meeting. If for any reason the annual meeting is not held in any year, the Board may transact at any subsequent regular or special meeting any business that could have been transacted at the annual meeting, and the failure to hold an annual meeting in any year shall not affect the validity of any corporate action.

### §11. Regular Meetings

The Board may hold regular meetings at such times and places (which may be physical, virtual, or hybrid) as the Board may from time to time determine by resolution. No notice of regular meetings is required other than the resolution establishing the meeting schedule.

### §12. Special Meetings

Special meetings of the Board may be called by (a) the Chair, (b) any two (2) Directors acting together, or (c) a majority of the Directors then in office, in each case by delivery of notice in accordance with §13.

### §13. Notice of Meetings

§13.1. **Notice required.** Notice of each special meeting of the Board shall be given to each Director not less than five (5) days before the meeting if given by mail, or not less than forty-eight (48) hours before the meeting if given by electronic transmission, telephone, or hand delivery. Notice shall state the date, time, and place (or means of remote participation) of the meeting and the general nature of the business to be transacted.

§13.2. **Waiver.** A Director may waive notice of any meeting by written waiver, by electronic transmission, or by attendance at the meeting (other than attendance for the express purpose of objecting at the beginning of the meeting to the transaction of business because the meeting was not lawfully called or convened), in accordance with Section 229 of the DGCL.

§13.3. **Annual and regular meetings.** No separate notice is required for the annual meeting or for any regular meeting held in accordance with a schedule adopted by the Board.

### §14. Quorum

§14.1. **Quorum.** A majority of the Directors then in office shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business at any meeting of the Board.

§14.2. **Loss of quorum during meeting.** If a quorum is present at the commencement of a meeting but is subsequently lost, the remaining Directors may continue to transact business only with the consent of a majority of the full Board (which may be obtained at the meeting or by reasonable subsequent communication, including electronic communication, in accordance with §17).

§14.3. **Adjournment for lack of quorum.** If a quorum is not present at any meeting of the Board, a majority of the Directors present may adjourn the meeting from time to time without further notice (other than announcement at the meeting) until a quorum is obtained.

§14.4. **Effect of recusal on quorum.** If, by reason of recusal under the COI Policy, a sufficient number of Directors must recuse to cause the remaining non-recused Directors to fall below the quorum requirement under §14.1, the matter shall be considered by the Board only with the recused Directors absent, and the remaining non-recused Directors shall constitute a quorum for the limited purpose of acting on that matter, in accordance with Section 144 of the DGCL. Section 144(a)(1) of the DGCL expressly provides that an interested-Director transaction may be approved by a majority of the disinterested Directors even if those disinterested Directors are less than a quorum. The action of a majority of the remaining non-recused Directors shall be the act of the Board for purposes of that matter, provided that no fewer than two (2) non-recused Directors participate in the deliberation and vote.

### §15. Voting

The act of a majority of the Directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board, except where a greater vote is required by the DGCL, the Certificate, these Bylaws, or any policy adopted by the Board pursuant to authority granted in these Bylaws.

### §16. Action by Written Consent

§16.1. **General rule.** Any action required or permitted to be taken at any meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting by Written Consent in accordance with this §16 and Section 141(f) of the DGCL.

§16.2. **Unanimous Written Consent — default.** Except as provided in §16.3, action by Written Consent shall require the written consent of all Directors then in office.

§16.3. **Supermajority Written Consent — Grant approvals.** Action by Written Consent on the approval of a Grant under the Corporation's Grantmaking Policy may be taken by the affirmative Written Consent of two-thirds (2/3) of the Directors then in office, in accordance with the Written Consent approval procedures of the Grantmaking Policy. The Written Consent procedures of the Grantmaking Policy, including any Director's right to require meeting consideration of a Grant proposed for Written Consent, govern Grant approvals taken under this §16.3.

§16.4. **Form of Written Consent.** A Written Consent under this §16 may be in any tangible or electronic form (including consent transmitted by email or through any electronic platform reasonably designated by the Board) and shall be filed with the minutes of the proceedings of the Board.

§16.5. **Effective date.** Action by Written Consent shall be effective when the required number of Directors have signed the consent, unless the consent specifies a later effective date.


### §17. Telephonic / Video Meetings

In accordance with Section 141(i) of the DGCL, Directors may participate in any meeting of the Board, or any committee thereof, by means of conference telephone, video conference, or any other communications equipment by which all persons participating in the meeting can hear each other. Participation by such means constitutes presence in person at the meeting. Meetings may be held entirely by such means.

### §18. Conduct of Meetings

The Chair, or in the Chair's absence such other Director as the Directors present may designate, shall preside at all meetings of the Board. The Secretary, or in the Secretary's absence such other person as the presiding officer may designate, shall record the minutes of the meeting. The Board may adopt such rules of procedure as it considers appropriate, and in the absence of such rules, the presiding officer shall determine matters of procedure consistent with these Bylaws and applicable law.

## Article IV — Officers

### §1. Required Officers

§1.1. The officers of the Corporation shall consist of a Chair, a Secretary, a Treasurer, and a Grants Lead, each of whom shall be elected or designated by the Board. The Board may, by resolution under §8, elect or appoint additional officers (including, by way of example, a Vice Chair, one or more Vice Presidents, or assistant officers) as it deems appropriate.

§1.2. Any one person may hold more than one office, except that:

- no person may simultaneously hold the offices of Chair and Secretary; and
- no person may simultaneously hold the offices of Grants Lead and Treasurer.


### §2. Election and Term

§2.1. **Election.** The Board shall elect officers at the annual meeting of the Board held under Article III, §10, or at such other meeting as the Board may designate.

§2.2. **Term.** Each officer shall serve a term of one (1) year and until such officer's successor is elected and qualified, or until such officer's earlier death, resignation, or removal.

§2.3. **Eligibility.** Each officer shall be a natural person of full legal age. The Chair, Treasurer, and Grants Lead must be Directors. The Secretary need not be a Director. No person shall be eligible to serve as an officer of the Corporation if such person appears on the Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List maintained by OFAC or on any other list of persons with whom the Corporation is prohibited from engaging in transactions under U.S. economic sanctions laws. Each officer shall promptly notify the Board if the officer becomes aware of any fact that would, or reasonably might, affect the officer's qualification under the preceding sentence; upon such notification, the officer shall be deemed to have automatically resigned, and the resulting vacancy shall be filled in accordance with §3.3 of this Article IV.


### §3. Removal and Vacancies

§3.1. **Removal.** Any officer may be removed at any time, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of a majority of the Directors then in office. Removal shall be without prejudice to any contract rights of the officer (if any), and election or appointment of an officer shall not of itself create contract rights.

§3.2. **Resignation.** Any officer may resign at any time by delivering written notice to the Board Chair or the Secretary (or, in the case of resignation by the Chair, to any other Director). The resignation shall be effective upon receipt unless the notice specifies a later effective date.

§3.3. **Vacancies.** Any vacancy in any office may be filled by the Board for the unexpired portion of the term.

### §4. Chair

§4.1. The Chair shall preside at all meetings of the Board and shall be the principal officer of the Corporation for purposes of presiding and signing documents requiring an executive signature on behalf of the Corporation.

§4.2. The Chair shall perform such other duties as the Board may from time to time prescribe.

### §5. Secretary

§5.1. The Secretary shall:

- keep, or cause to be kept, the minutes of all meetings of the Board and of any committees of the Board;
- give, or cause to be given, all notices required by these Bylaws or applicable law;
- be the custodian of the corporate records of the Corporation, including the minute books, the Bylaws, the Certificate, the records of policies adopted by the Board, the records of executed Conflict of Interest disclosures under the COI Policy, the Director Nomination Process records, and other documents pertaining to the governance of the Corporation; and
- have signing authority for routine documents on behalf of the Corporation as authorized by the Board.

§5.2. The Secretary shall perform such other duties as the Board or the Chair may from time to time prescribe.

### §6. Treasurer

§6.1. The Treasurer shall have general oversight of the financial affairs of the Corporation, including the receipt, custody, and disbursement of the Corporation's funds and assets, in coordination with such staff and outside service providers as the Board may engage.

§6.2. The Treasurer shall have signing authority on financial documents and accounts on behalf of the Corporation as authorized by the Board, including authority over banking relationships and on-chain custody arrangements consistent with Article IX.

§6.3. The Treasurer shall present financial reports to the Board at each regular meeting and shall present audited or reviewed financial statements (when available) at the annual meeting under Article VIII, §4.

§6.4. The Treasurer shall perform such other duties as the Board or the Chair may from time to time prescribe.

### §7. Grants Lead

§7.1. **Designation and term.** The Grants Lead shall be designated by the Board for a term of one (1) year, with rotation flexibility. The Board may redesignate the same person for additional one-year terms or rotate the role among eligible Officers.

§7.2. **Duties.** The Grants Lead is responsible for the operational coordination of the Corporation's grantmaking program. The duties of the Grants Lead, including initial screening of Charitable Recommendation Proposals, coordination of due diligence, preparation of grant memos, distribution of Written Consent packages, and reporting to the Board, are set forth in the Grants Lead responsibilities provisions of the Grantmaking Policy. The Grants Lead has no independent authority to approve, decline, or modify any Grant; all Grant approvals require Board action under the Board approval provisions of the Grantmaking Policy.

§7.3. **Compensation.** The Grants Lead is eligible for compensation under the Compensation Review Process if the Board so determines, subject to §9.

### §8. Other Officers

The Board may, by resolution, create such additional officer positions (including one or more assistant officers) as it deems appropriate, with such titles, duties, and authorities as the Board may prescribe.

### §9. Compensation

Officer compensation, if any, shall be reviewed and approved through a procedure designed to qualify for the rebuttable presumption of reasonableness under Section 4958 of the Internal Revenue Code and Treasury Regulation § 53.4958-6. Section 4958 imposes excise taxes on compensation paid by the Corporation to a "Disqualified Person" (any officer, Director, or other person in a position to exercise substantial influence over the Corporation's affairs) to the extent the compensation exceeds reasonable value, and on any organization manager who knowingly approves such an arrangement. The rebuttable presumption procedure provides a safe harbor that, when followed, causes the IRS to presume the compensation is reasonable, shifting the burden of proving unreasonableness to the IRS. The procedure requires:

- approval by an independent body free of conflicts;
- reliance on appropriate comparability data; and
- contemporaneous documentation of the basis for the determination.

The operational details of the procedure are set forth in the Compensation Arrangements provisions of the COI Policy and the Compensation Review Process. This §9 applies to all officer positions, including the Grants Lead under §7.


### §10. Bonding

The Board may require any officer or employee of the Corporation to provide a bond, in such amount and with such surety as the Board may determine, conditioned on the faithful performance of the duties of the officer's or employee's position. The cost of any such bond shall be borne by the Corporation.

### §11. Delegation

In the case of absence or inability to act of any officer of the Corporation, the Board may delegate, for the time being, the powers and duties of such officer to any other officer or to any Director.

## Article V — Committees

### §1. Discretionary Committees

The Board may, by resolution, establish such standing or ad hoc committees as it deems appropriate, including (without limitation) an Audit and Risk Committee, an Investment Committee, a Compensation Committee, a Grants Committee, and a Governance Committee. The composition, charter, and responsibilities of any such committee shall be set forth in the resolution establishing the committee or in a separate charter adopted by the Board. Cross-references to such committees in the COI Policy, the Compensation Review Process, the Grantmaking Policy, and other Board-adopted policies shall be operative if and when the relevant committee is established; absent such a committee, the responsibilities described in those policies shall be performed by the full Board, by the Grants Lead under Article IV, §7, or by such other body or Director(s) as the Board may designate.


### §2. Committee Authority

§2.1. **General delegation.** A committee, to the extent provided in the resolution of the Board establishing the committee or in a charter adopted by the Board, may exercise the powers and authority of the Board in the management of the business and affairs of the Corporation, in accordance with Section 141(c)(2) of the DGCL.

§2.2. **Reserved actions.** No committee shall have the power or authority to take any action that, under the DGCL, the Certificate, or these Bylaws, is reserved to the Board, including (without limitation):

- amending the Certificate (except to the limited extent permitted under Section 151(a) of the DGCL, which is inapplicable to the Corporation);
- adopting, amending, or repealing these Bylaws;
- approving any merger, consolidation, conversion, transfer, or domestication;
- approving the dissolution of the Corporation or the distribution of substantially all of its assets;
- electing, removing, or filling vacancies among Directors; or
- electing or removing officers (other than as may be specifically delegated by the Board).

### §3. Committee Procedures

Each committee shall follow the procedural rules set forth in Article III with respect to meetings, notice, quorum, voting, action by Written Consent (subject to the Written Consent restrictions in Article III, §16), and telephonic/video participation, except to the extent the committee's charter, adopted by the Board, provides otherwise.

### §4. Composition Restrictions

Every committee that exercises any portion of the authority of the Board shall include a majority of Directors who are Independent Directors. Non-Director members may serve on committees only in an advisory or non-voting capacity unless the resolution establishing the committee or the committee's charter specifies otherwise consistent with Section 141(c) of the DGCL.

## Article VI — Indemnification and Insurance

### §1. Indemnification of Directors and Officers

§1.1. **Mandatory indemnification.** The Corporation shall, to the fullest extent permitted by Section 145 of the DGCL (as it now exists or may hereafter be amended, but in the case of any such amendment, only to the extent that such amendment permits the Corporation to provide broader indemnification rights than were permitted prior to the amendment), indemnify each person who was or is made a party or is threatened to be made a party to or is otherwise involved in any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative (a "**Proceeding**"), by reason of the fact that such person is or was a Director or officer of the Corporation, or, while a Director or officer of the Corporation, is or was serving at the request of the Corporation as a director, officer, employee, or agent of another corporation or of a partnership, joint venture, trust, or other enterprise, against all expense, liability, and loss (including attorneys' fees, judgments, fines, ERISA excise taxes or penalties, and amounts paid in settlement) reasonably incurred or suffered by such person in connection with the Proceeding.

§1.2. **Third-party and derivative actions.** Indemnification under this §1 covers both Proceedings brought by third parties under Section 145(a) of the DGCL and Proceedings brought by or in the right of the Corporation under Section 145(b) of the DGCL, in each case to the fullest extent permitted by such provisions.

§1.3. **Independent Director investigation prerequisite to advancement.** Prior to advancement of expenses to a Director or officer under §3, the Board shall, by majority vote of the Directors not party to the Proceeding (or, if there are no such Directors, by independent legal counsel selected by the Board), determine that such advancement is appropriate under the circumstances. This determination is procedural and does not constitute a determination of the underlying entitlement to indemnification, which shall be made in accordance with Section 145.

### §2. Indemnification of Employees and Agents

The Corporation may, by resolution of the Board, indemnify and advance expenses to any employee or agent of the Corporation to the fullest extent permitted by Section 145 of the DGCL. The Board's authority under this §2 is permissive and discretionary.

### §3. Advancement of Expenses

The Corporation shall pay the expenses (including attorneys' fees) reasonably incurred by a Director or officer in defending any Proceeding in advance of its final disposition, provided that such advancement of expenses shall be made only upon delivery to the Corporation of an undertaking by or on behalf of such Director or officer to repay all amounts so advanced if it shall ultimately be determined by final judicial decision from which there is no further right to appeal that such Director or officer is not entitled to be indemnified for such expenses under §1 or otherwise. Advancement under this §3 is subject to the Independent Director investigation prerequisite under §1.3.

### §4. Non-Exclusivity

The indemnification and advancement of expenses provided by this Article VI shall not be deemed exclusive of any other rights to which any person may be entitled under the Certificate, any agreement, vote of disinterested Directors, applicable law, or otherwise, both as to action in such person's official capacity and as to action in another capacity while holding such office, in accordance with Section 145(f) of the DGCL.

### §5. Insurance

In accordance with Section 145(g) of the DGCL, the Corporation may purchase and maintain insurance, at the Corporation's expense, on behalf of any person who is or was a Director, officer, employee, or agent of the Corporation, or is or was serving at the request of the Corporation as a director, officer, employee, or agent of another enterprise, against any liability asserted against such person and incurred by such person in any such capacity, or arising out of such person's status as such, whether or not the Corporation would have the power to indemnify such person against such liability under the provisions of this Article VI or Section 145.

### §6. Continuation

The indemnification and advancement of expenses provided by this Article VI shall continue as to a person who has ceased to be a Director, officer, employee, or agent of the Corporation, and shall inure to the benefit of the heirs, executors, administrators, and personal representatives of such person.

### §7. Exclusions

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Article VI:

§7.1. The Corporation shall not indemnify any Director or officer with respect to any Proceeding to the extent that indemnification is prohibited by Section 145 of the DGCL, including the limitations in Sections 145(a) and 145(b) requiring that the person to be indemnified acted in good faith and in a manner the person reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Corporation, and (with respect to criminal Proceedings) had no reasonable cause to believe the person's conduct was unlawful.

§7.2. The Corporation shall not indemnify any Director or officer with respect to any Proceeding (or part thereof) initiated by such person against the Corporation or its Directors, officers, employees, or agents, unless the Proceeding (or part thereof) was authorized in advance by the Board.

§7.3. Recognizing that the assets of the Corporation are held in trust for charitable purposes, the Corporation shall not indemnify any Director, officer, employee, or agent for any action or omission that the Board determines:

- constituted willful or intentional misconduct, willful or intentional violation of law, knowing violation of the requirements applicable to a Section 501(c)(3) organization, or self-dealing in violation of Sections 4941 or 4958 of the Code; or
- would jeopardize the Corporation's tax-exempt status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code if indemnification were provided.

This §7.3 is intended to satisfy any limitations on indemnification of charitable corporations under Delaware law and to preserve the Corporation's tax-exempt status.

## Article VII — Conflicts of Interest

### §1. Adoption of COI Policy

The Board shall adopt, maintain, and enforce a written Conflict of Interest Policy substantially in the form of the Conflict of Interest Policy adopted contemporaneously with these Bylaws (the "**COI Policy**"). The COI Policy shall be administered in accordance with its terms, and the substantive procedures of the COI Policy are incorporated by reference into these Bylaws.

### §2. Annual COI Statements

Each Director, officer, and Key Employee (as defined in the COI Policy) shall annually execute the Annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form required under the Annual Statements provisions of the COI Policy, and shall otherwise comply with the duty-to-disclose provisions and the standing-disclosure-of-Nouns-affiliation provisions of the COI Policy.

### §3. Recusal

The recusal definition and the recusal-procedure provisions of the COI Policy apply to all Board and committee actions of the Corporation. A Director, officer, or Key Employee with a Conflict of Interest with respect to any matter shall recuse from the matter in accordance with the COI Policy and shall not vote on the matter. The effect of recusal on quorum is governed by Article III, §14.4.

## Article VIII — Records, Reports, and Inspection

### §1. Records to be Maintained

The Corporation shall maintain, at its principal place of business or at such other location(s) as the Board may designate (which may be electronic):

§1.1. The Certificate, these Bylaws, and all amendments thereto;

§1.2. The minutes of all meetings of the Board and of each committee of the Board, and all Written Consents executed by the Board or any committee;

§1.3. The financial records of the Corporation, including its books of account, banking records, and on-chain treasury records;

§1.4. Records of all Conflict of Interest disclosures executed under the COI Policy, all recusal records, and all records of the annual periodic reviews conducted under the COI Policy;

§1.5. Records of each Grant made by the Corporation, including diligence files, grant agreements, and reports as required under the documentation and record-keeping provisions of the Grantmaking Policy;

§1.6. Records of each Director Nomination Proposal received and considered under Article III, §5 and the Director Nomination Policy;

§1.7. Records of all federal, state, and local tax filings, including Form 990, Form 1023, the Corporation's federal tax-exemption determination letter, and any state charitable registration filings; and

§1.8. Such other records as the Board may from time to time direct or as may be required by applicable law.

The records described in this §1 shall be retained in accordance with the Corporation's Document Retention Policy when adopted.

### §2. Inspection by Directors

Every Director shall have the absolute right at any reasonable time to inspect all books, records, and documents of every kind of the Corporation, and the physical or electronic properties of the Corporation, in accordance with the duties of the Director under Delaware law. The right of inspection includes the right to make copies and extracts. The Corporation may impose reasonable confidentiality requirements on inspection by Directors with respect to records the Board has determined to be confidential, provided that no such requirement shall impair a Director's right to information necessary for the discharge of the Director's fiduciary duties.

### §3. Public Inspection of Tax Documents

The Corporation shall make available for public inspection, in accordance with Section 6104 of the Code and the regulations thereunder, copies of (a) its application for tax exemption (Form 1023) and any supporting documents filed with the Internal Revenue Service, (b) its federal tax-exemption determination letter, and (c) its three (3) most recent annual information returns (Form 990 or Form 990-EZ, as applicable), and shall furnish copies upon request in accordance with applicable law.

### §4. Annual Report to Board

At each annual meeting of the Board, the Treasurer shall present, or cause to be presented, the audited or reviewed financial statements of the Corporation for the prior fiscal year (when available), together with such other financial information as the Board may from time to time require. If audited or reviewed financial statements are not yet available at the time of the annual meeting, the Treasurer shall present the most current internal financial reports and shall present the audited or reviewed statements at the next regular meeting following their availability.

### §5. Records Custodian

The Secretary shall be the custodian of the corporate records of the Corporation, in accordance with Article IV, §6, and shall be responsible for the maintenance and accessibility of those records consistent with this Article VIII and the Corporation's Document Retention Policy.

## Article IX — Fiscal Matters

### §1. Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year, unless otherwise established by resolution of the Board.

### §2. Banking and Custody

§2.1. **Authorization.** The Board may authorize the Corporation to establish and maintain accounts at one or more financial institutions and to establish and maintain on-chain treasury arrangements at one or more digital-asset custody platforms or smart-contract-based custody arrangements, in each case for the receipt, custody, and disbursement of the Corporation's funds and assets.

§2.2. **Multisig requirement for on-chain treasury.** Any on-chain treasury arrangement of the Corporation shall be structured as a multi-signature arrangement requiring the affirmative authorization of not fewer than three (3) of five (5) designated signers (or such other multi-signature configuration as the Board may from time to time approve, provided that no single signer shall have unilateral authority to disburse Corporation funds or assets). The Board shall designate the signers by resolution and may modify the designation from time to time.

§2.3. **Designation of signing authorities.** The Board shall designate the Officers and other persons authorized to sign on the Corporation's bank accounts and to sign or authorize on-chain transactions, and shall establish appropriate dual-signature, multisig, or other authorization requirements consistent with the Corporation's risk profile.


### §3. Contracts and Authorization

§3.1. **General authorization.** The Board may, by resolution, authorize any officer or other person to enter into any contract or execute any instrument on behalf of the Corporation. Such authority may be general or confined to specific instances.

§3.2. **Routine contracts.** Routine contracts within the budget approved by the Board may be authorized and executed by officers of the Corporation in the ordinary course, without specific Board action, in accordance with policies and authorization thresholds the Board may from time to time adopt.

### §4. Loans and Borrowing

§4.1. **Prohibition on insider loans.** The Corporation shall not make any loan to, or accept any loan from, any Director, officer, or Family Member (as defined in the COI Policy) of any Director or officer.

§4.2. **Third-party loans.** The Corporation may make or accept loans to or from third parties only with specific authorization by the Board.

### §5. Checks and Payment Authorizations

The Board shall designate the persons authorized to sign checks, drafts, wire transfers, and other instruments for the payment of money on behalf of the Corporation, and shall establish appropriate authorization thresholds. On-chain payment authorizations shall be subject to the multisig requirements of §2.2.

### §6. Investment of Assets

The investment of the Corporation's assets shall be conducted in accordance with the Investment Policy Statement adopted by the Board (when adopted). Pending adoption of an Investment Policy Statement, the investment authority of the Corporation vests in the Treasurer, subject to the oversight of the Board and the requirements of Delaware law applicable to the investment of charitable assets, including the Delaware Uniform Prudent Management of Institutional Funds Act (12 Del. C. §§ 4701 et seq.) to the extent applicable.

## Article X — Amendments and Miscellaneous

### §1. Amendment of Bylaws

§1.1. **Power to amend.** In accordance with Section 109 of the DGCL, the Board has the power to adopt, amend, alter, or repeal these Bylaws.

§1.2. **Standard threshold.** Except as provided in §1.3, an amendment, alteration, or repeal of these Bylaws requires the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the Directors then in office at any regular or special meeting of the Board for which the proposed amendment, alteration, or repeal has been included in the notice of the meeting (or as to which notice has been waived).

§1.3. **Heightened threshold provisions.** Amendments to Article III, §5 (Director Nomination Process) require the affirmative vote and notice procedures specified in Article III, §5.6, which control over the standard threshold of §1.2.


### §2. Amendment of Certificate

The Certificate may be amended in accordance with Section 242 of the DGCL. Because the Corporation has no members, an amendment to the Certificate may be authorized by resolution of the Board adopted by a majority of the Directors then in office (or such greater vote as the Certificate or these Bylaws may require for a particular type of amendment), in accordance with Section 242(b)(3) of the DGCL.

### §3. Conflict with Certificate

In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between these Bylaws and the Certificate, the Certificate shall control.

### §4. Conflict with Law

In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between these Bylaws and the DGCL or other applicable law, the DGCL or other applicable law shall control. Any provision of these Bylaws determined to be invalid or unenforceable shall be severable, and the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### §5. Construction

In these Bylaws: (a) the singular includes the plural and the plural includes the singular; (b) words denoting any gender include all genders and gender-neutral references; (c) the words "include," "includes," and "including" are deemed to be followed by "without limitation"; (d) references to a Section, Article, Subsection, or paragraph are references to Sections, Articles, Subsections, or paragraphs of these Bylaws unless otherwise specified; (e) references to a statutory provision include the provision as amended from time to time and any successor provision; and (f) headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

### §6. Notice

Any notice required to be given under these Bylaws may be given by hand delivery, by mail (in which case notice is effective on the earlier of receipt or three (3) business days after deposit in the mail with first-class postage prepaid, addressed to the recipient at the recipient's address shown on the records of the Corporation), or by electronic transmission (including email), in accordance with Section 232 of the DGCL. A Director who has consented to receive notice by electronic transmission may revoke that consent in accordance with Section 232 of the DGCL.

### §7. Waiver of Notice

Whenever any notice is required to be given under the DGCL, the Certificate, or these Bylaws, a written waiver thereof, signed by the person entitled to such notice, whether before or after the time stated therein, shall be deemed equivalent to such notice, in accordance with Section 229 of the DGCL. Attendance of a Director at any meeting of the Board (or a committee thereof) constitutes a waiver of notice of such meeting, except where the Director attends a meeting for the express purpose of objecting at the beginning of the meeting to the transaction of business because the meeting is not lawfully called or convened.

### §8. Dissolution

§8.1. **Distribution on dissolution.** Upon the dissolution of the Corporation, after paying or making provision for the payment of all of the liabilities of the Corporation, the remaining assets of the Corporation shall be distributed exclusively for the purposes described in §4 of Article I, in such manner and to such organization or organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, or other purposes described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Code as the Board shall determine. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware, for such purposes or to such organization or organizations as such court shall determine to be organized and operated exclusively for such purposes.

§8.2. **Coordination with Certificate.** This §8 is intended to coordinate with, and not to override, the dissolution clause of the Certificate. In the event of any inconsistency, the Certificate shall control under §3.
